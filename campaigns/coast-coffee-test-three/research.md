## 1. Customer Research

### Framework: Jobs-to-be-Done (Christensen) + Pain-Point Mapping

| Job | Context | Functional Job | Emotional Job | Social Job |
|-----|---------|---------------|---------------|------------|

#### 1.2 Pain Points (from DNA, with inferred depth)

| Pain Point | Severity (inferred) | Current alternatives customers use | Coast Coffee's counter |
|-----------|---------------------|-----------------------------------|------------------------|
| **Freshness uncertainty by mail** — "will the beans actually be fresh when they arrive?" | HIGH — core trust issue for mail-order beans | Buy from physical roasters; check roast dates obsessively; subscription services with freshness claims | Tested + degassed + vacuum-sealed + ≥6-week guarantee |
| **Paradox of choice** — too many origins, don't know what to pick | MEDIUM-HIGH — leads to decision paralysis or safe repeats | Stick to known origins (Ethiopia, Colombia); rely on barista recommendations in-store | 3-tier flavour framework removes guesswork |
| **Unfamiliar Asian origins** — "are they as good as the African/Latin beans I know?" | MEDIUM — curiosity tempered by scepticism | Avoid Asian origins; stick to specialty-shop favourites | Curated tasting, tier system signals quality control |
| **Price objection** — "why pay SGD 18–30 when supermarket is cheaper?" | MEDIUM — but specialty buyers already self-select past this | Buy supermarket beans for daily; treat specialty as occasional | Tier system + trio offer communicates value; freshness justifies premium |
| **Trust in a new brand** — "can I trust a small, unknown brand?" | HIGH for first purchase | Buy from established roasters with reputation; look for social proof | Freshness guarantee, testing process, vacuum-seal as tangible proof points |

#### 1.3 Purchase Decision Chain (inferred)

```
Trigger (running low / want to try something new)
  → Research (social media, Google, word of mouth, specialty forums)
  → Evaluate (origin? freshness? price? reviews? curation?)
  → Decide (trust signal + value signal must both be met)
  → Buy (low-friction checkout, delivery expectation set)
  → Receive & Brew (freshness promise tested immediately)
  → Repeat or churn (first-bag experience determines lifetime value)
```

## 5. Audience Segmentation

### Framework: Needs-Based Segmentation (primary) + Behavioural Overlay

The DNA identifies one primary segment: **Singapore home baristas who want curated, guaranteed-fresh Asian-origin beans delivered**. Within this, we can identify four meaningful sub-segments based on brewing behaviour and psychological need:

#### Segment A: "The Safe Brewer" (~35% of target)
- **Profile:** Brews daily but sticks to what they know. Owns a V60 or French press. Buys the same 1–2 origins repeatedly. Curious about Asian beans but worried about wasting money.
- **Dominant need:** Risk reduction — "don't let me buy a bag I'll hate."
- **JTBD priority:** Get reliably good coffee every morning without surprises.
- **Entry point:** Tier 1 (safe/basic) — "tastes like what you already love, but from an origin you haven't tried."
- **Objection to overcome:** "Why try something new when I know what works?"

#### Segment B: "The Flavour Explorer" (~30% of target)
- **Profile:** Brews 3–5×/week with multiple methods (pour-over, Aeropress, maybe entry-level espresso). Actively seeks new origins. Reads tasting notes. Follows roasters on Instagram.
- **Dominant need:** Discovery with guardrails — "surprise me, but don't disappoint me."
- **JTBD priority:** Expand my palate with interesting beans someone knowledgeable has vetted.
- **Entry point:** Tier 2 (complex/floral) — "the sweet spot between familiar and adventurous."
- **Objection to overcome:** "Are Asian origins genuinely interesting, or just a gimmick?"

#### Segment C: "The Identity Brewer" (~20% of target)
- **Profile:** Coffee is part of their identity. Owns prosumer espresso gear or a full pour-over ritual. Shares brew photos. Wants beans with a story — origin, producer, processing method.
- **Dominant need:** Status and uniqueness — "I want beans nobody else has."
- **JTBD priority:** Serve and share coffee that reflects my taste and knowledge.
- **Entry point:** Tier 3 (exciting/unknown) — "the beans even most specialty shops don't stock."
- **Objection to overcome:** "Is this genuinely rare/special, or just marketed that way?"

#### Segment D: "The Convenience Seeker" (~15% of target)
- **Profile:** Enjoys good coffee but won't travel to get it. Time-poor. Buys online. Wants the quality of a roaster with the ease of delivery. May use a subscription service already.
- **Dominant need:** Friction reduction — "make it easy to get good beans."
- **JTBD priority:** Restock quality beans without leaving home or overthinking.
- **Entry point:** All three tiers — pick the flavour profile, we deliver.
- **Objection to overcome:** "Why switch from my current delivery/subscription?"

#### Segment Prioritisation for This Campaign

| Segment | Estimated size | Conversion ease | LTV potential | Priority |
|---------|---------------|-----------------|---------------|----------|
| A — Safe Brewer | Largest (~35%) | EASIEST — Tier 1 beans are the lowest barrier | MODERATE — may not explore beyond Tier 1 | HIGH |
| B — Flavour Explorer | Large (~30%) | MODERATE — need to prove Asian origins are interesting | HIGH — likely to try all three tiers over time | HIGHEST |
| C — Identity Brewer | Smaller (~20%) | HARDEST — high bar for credibility | HIGHEST — most loyal, best word-of-mouth | SECONDARY |
| D — Convenience Seeker | Smallest (~15%) | MODERATE — convenience alone isn't a moat | LOW — will switch again for better convenience | TERTIARY |

**Recommendation grounded in DNA:** The trio offer (one bag from each tier, ~SGD 50–54) is best positioned for Segments A and B — it gives Safe Brewers a permission slip to explore (with two "safe" anchors) and gives Flavour Explorers the variety they crave with a Tier 3 "bonus" that feels exclusive.

**Gap:** Segment sizes are inferred, not data-backed. Validate with a survey of Singapore home baristas or by analysing social media interest-group sizes.
