## Executive Summary

- **Total paid budget:** SGD 1,000 over 8 weeks (~SGD 125/week).
- **Primary paid channel:** Instagram (paid) — 55% of budget. Best format for the lead freshness/origin video (Creative KPI: hook rate ≥ 25%); visual storytelling for the 3-tier framework; builds retargeting pool. [RESEARCH §5.1]
- **Secondary paid channel:** Google Search — 30% of budget. Captures bottom-funnel high-intent buyers searching "buy coffee beans Singapore." [RESEARCH §5.1]
- **Retargeting:** Facebook — 10% of budget. Recovers warm traffic from landing-page visitors.
- **Experimental:** Reddit/Telegram — 5% of budget. Low-cost test for organic community seeding.
- **Key principle:** Budget concentrated on 2 primary channels + 1 retarget, not spread thin — necessary for SGD 1,000 where each dollar must earn its place.
- **Expected outcome:** Paid drives ~28–63 conversions; organic (editorial PR, organic social, word-of-mouth) drives remaining ~25–35 → blended total ~70 → blended CAC ~SGD 14.3 (within margin of ≤ SGD 14 target).

## 2. Test Structure (Weeks 3-5 — Launch Phase)

### 2.1 Variant Tests

| Test | Variant A | Variant B | Success Read | Channel | Duration |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Lead video hook** | Freshness hook (ASMR vacuum-seal hiss → "Most coffee is fresh for days. Ours is fresh for weeks.") | Curiosity hook ("Where does your coffee come from?" → Asian-origin discovery angle) | Hook rate ≥ 25%; CTR ≥ 1.0% | Instagram paid | Week 3 only (50/50 split, then pick winner for weeks 4-5) |
| **Landing page CTA** | "Get the Coast Trio" (action-driven) | "Find Your Flavour" (discovery-driven) | CVR ≥ 3% | Landing page | Weeks 3-4 (50/50 split via A/B test tool) |
| **Audience — interest targeting** | "Coffee" + "Specialty coffee" broad interest | "Home barista" + "Pour-over" + "Espresso machine" narrow interest | CPP ≤ SGD 25 | Instagram paid | Week 3 only (50/50 split) |
| **Retargeting audience window** | 7-day retargeting window | 14-day retargeting window | CPC ≤ SGD 0.80; CVR ≥ 5% | Facebook retarget | Weeks 5-8 |

### 2.2 How Success Is Read

- **After Week 3:** Compare hook rate, CTR, and CPP for the two video hooks. The winner gets 70% of Instagram paid budget for weeks 4-5.
- **After Week 4:** Compare CVR for the two landing page CTAs. Winner stays; loser is dropped.
- **After Week 5:** Compare performance of broad vs. narrow interest targeting. The better-performing audience gets the remaining Instagram budget.
- **After Week 7:** Evaluate whether 7-day or 14-day retargeting window delivers lower CPP. Use the winner for the final week.

### 3.2 Business KPIs

| KPI | Target | How It's Measured | Why This Target | Evidence Trace |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **New customers (first-time buyers)** | **70 in 8 weeks** | E-commerce platform unique first-purchase count | Primary business objective. 70 customers in 8 weeks from ~15K–30K addressable market = ~0.2–0.5% capture — achievable. | [Goal; RESEARCH §3.3] |
| **Blended CAC** | **≤ SGD 14** | Total paid spend (SGD 1,000) ÷ total new customers | Paid expected to drive ~35–45 conversions; organic drives remainder. CAC of SGD 14 implies 71 customers at SGD 1,000 total. | [Goal; RESEARCH §5.2] |
| **AOV** | **≥ SGD 50** | Total revenue ÷ total orders | Trio offer at SGD 50–54 supports this directly. Single-bag orders (SGD 18–30) would drag AOV down — trio must be the primary first-purchase option. | [Goal; RESEARCH §6] |
| **Repeat purchase rate (weeks 9-12)** | **≥ 15% within 4 weeks** | % of first-time buyers who purchase again within 4 weeks | Indicates product-market fit and trio→subscription funnel potential. | [INFERENCE — standard DTC retention benchmark] |

### 3.5 KPI Tracking Cadence

| KPI | Review Frequency | Owner | Decision if Below Target |
|---|---|---|---|
| **New customers (weekly)** | Weekly (every Monday) | Marketing Director | Investigate channel underperformance; reallocate budget from worst channel to best |
| **Blended CAC** | Weekly | Marketing Director | If CAC > SGD 14 after week 4, reduce paid spend and increase organic/PR effort |
| **Landing-page CVR** | Daily (first 2 weeks), then weekly | Marketing Director / Performance | If CVR < 3%, A/B test: quiz placement, trio price visibility, guarantee prominence |
| **CPP on paid** | Weekly | Performance Marketing | If CPP > SGD 25, pause worst-performing ad set; shift budget to better performer |
| **Hook rate** | Per creative launch (at least weekly in Launch phase) | Creative Director | If hook rate < 25%, re-edit lead video: test different hooks (freshness vs. origin vs. adventurousness) |
| **Engagement rate** | Weekly (organic) | Content Manager / Creative Director | If engagement < 4%, adjust content mix: more educational carousels, more UGC, more interactive elements |

## 5. Key Risks & Mitigations

| Risk | Impact | Likelihood | Mitigation |
|---|---|---|---|
| **CVR below 3% on landing page** | Paid conversions drop from ~35 to ~23; blended CAC rises to ~SGD 19 | Medium | A/B test: move 3-tier quiz above the fold, add freshness guarantee badge near CTA, test trio price visibility |
| **Hook rate below 25% on lead video** | CTR drops → CPC rises → CPP rises above SGD 25 | Medium | Re-edit video with different hooks — test freshness (ASMR vacuum-seal) vs. curiosity vs. adventurousness |
| **Organic (editorial PR) delivers 0 customers** | Paid must deliver all 70, requiring CPP ≤ SGD 14.3 — unrealistic | Low-Medium | Pursue PR aggressively in Build phase. If no coverage by week 3, redirect organic effort to Instagram community building |
| **Trio economics force price above SGD 54** | Value message weakens; AOV target harder to hit | Unknown (customer must confirm) | If price must rise, adjust AOV target. Do not launch with trio price above SGD 60 without revisiting positioning |
| **Subscription expectation causes bounce** | Cold buyers expect subscription option (standard in market) and leave if absent | Medium | On landing page, add "Prefer a regular delivery? Join the waitlist for our upcoming subscription" |
| **No testimonials → trust objection kills conversion** | CVR below 2% as cold buyers don't trust an unknown brand | High (given DNA gap) | Front-load the guarantee + testing process on every page. Collect first-buyer reviews aggressively from week 3 onward. Consider "first 50 customers get a handwritten thank-you note" to encourage organic sharing |
