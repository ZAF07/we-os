# Intake Brief — Coast Coffee Launch

> Produced by Intake Agent. Sources: `customers/coast-coffee/dna.md`, `campaigns/coast-test/goal.md`.

---

## 1. Business & Customer

| Field | Value |
|---|---|
| **Business** | Coast Coffee — DTC specialty coffee beans sourced from Asian regions (China, Thailand, Indonesia, Malaysia, etc.) |
| **What they sell** | 200–250 g bags of tested, vacuum-sealed (after degassing) beans, organised under a curated 3-tier flavour system |
| **Price point** | SGD 18–30 per bag |
| **Category** | Direct-to-consumer specialty coffee; mail-order to home baristas |
| **Target segment (this campaign)** | Singapore home baristas who want curated, guaranteed-fresh Asian-origin beans matched to their flavour preference |
| **Geography** | Singapore (local) |
| **Language** | English (primary) |

---

## 2. Campaign Objective

| Field | Value |
|---|---|
| **Primary business objective** | Acquire **70 new customers** (first-time buyers) in 8 weeks |
| **Timeframe** | 8 weeks from launch |
| **Total paid budget** | SGD 1,000 (~SGD 125/week) |
| **Implicit blended CAC target** | ≤ SGD 14 (paid expected to drive ~35–45 of the 70; remainder organic) |

---

## 3. Success Metrics (Three KPI Tiers)

### Business KPI
- **70 new customers in 8 weeks**
- **Blended CAC ≤ SGD 14**
- **Secondary: AOV ≥ SGD 50** (via the trio offer)

### Marketing KPI
- Landing-page **conversion rate ≥ 3%**
- **Cost per purchase ≤ SGD 25** on paid channels
- **CTR ≥ 1.0%** on paid social

### Creative KPI
- **Hook rate ≥ 25%** (3-sec / thumb-stop) on the lead freshness/origin video
- **Engagement rate ≥ 4%** on organic posts

---

## 4. Offer / Promotion (Proposed — Validate)

An introductory **3-bean flavour trio** sampler:
- One bag from each tier (Tier 1 safe, Tier 2 complex-floral, Tier 3 exciting-unknown)
- Target price: ~SGD 50–54 (light bundle value vs. SGD 18–30/bag)
- Purpose: Low-risk discovery that showcases the curation system

> **⚠️ To validate:** Confirm trio economics (margin, shipping feasibility, whether this cannibalises single-bag purchases for first orders or encourages upsell later).

---

## 5. Hard Constraints (Non-Negotiable)

From DNA + Goal:

1. **Maximum 3 flavour variations** offered at a time (safe / complex-floral / exciting-unknown).
2. **Price band:** SGD 18–30 per 200–250 g bag.
3. **Freshness promise:** ≥6 weeks after degassing + vacuum-sealing — do not overstate.
4. **Standard Singapore F&B import/labelling compliance** — specifics to confirm.
5. **Budget cap:** SGD 1,000 total paid spend across 8 weeks.
6. **Target segment is fixed:** Singapore home baristas as described — no broadening without revisiting the goal.

---

## 6. Key Differentiators to Lean On (from DNA)

1. **3-tier flavour framework** — matched to adventurousness level, removes guesswork.
2. **Tested beans, vacuum-sealed after degassing, guaranteed ≥6 weeks freshness** — trust signal by mail.
3. **Asian-origin focus** (China, Thailand, Indonesia, Malaysia) — point of difference vs. typical African/Latin American beans.
4. **Home delivery** — solves the "can't travel to buy" pain point.
5. **Beans matched to individual barista profiles** — personalisation angle.

---

## 7. Inferred Common Objections (from DNA — Confirm)

| Objection | Implication for messaging |
|---|---|
| "Will the beans actually be fresh when they arrive?" | Freshness guarantee and vacuum-seal proof must be front-and-centre |
| "I don't know these Asian origins — are they as good as the origins I know?" | Need to educate + legitimise Asian-origin quality |
| "Why pay SGD 18–30 when the supermarket is cheaper?" | Value proposition = curation + freshness vs. commodity coffee |
| "Can I trust a new, small brand?" | Social proof, testimonials, and the testing process as trust signals |

---

## 8. Open Questions / Gaps (Require Customer Confirmation Before Deeper Work)

These are items the DNA marked as `<TO CONFIRM>` — they are **not** blockers for the intake brief itself but must be resolved before positioning/creative stages.

| # | Question | Why it matters |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | **Specific competitor names?** (Other Singapore specialty roasters / subscription services) | Without this, positioning risks being generic. |
| 2 | **Brand voice / tone guardrails?** (Do's and don'ts: formal vs. warm, tech-forward vs. artisan, etc.) | Without this, creative output may miss the brand's personality. |
| 3 | **Current channels & past results?** (Where does Coast Coffee market today? What has worked/failed?) | Without this, channel strategy is uninformed. |
| 4 | **Customer testimonials / proof assets?** (Existing reviews, ratings, success stories) | Without this, social proof is hard to deploy in creatives. |
| 5 | **Baseline metrics?** (Current orders/month, repeat rate, traffic, conversion rate) | Without this, we cannot measure campaign lift accurately. |
| 6 | **Singapore compliance specifics?** (F&B import/labelling — exact requirements) | Must not be wrong; legal risk. |
| 7 | **Trio offer economics?** (Cost of goods, margin at SGD 50–54, shipping cost for a 3-bag bundle) | Without this, the proposed offer may lose money or be unviable. |
| 8 | **Is Mandarin / Malay / Tamil reach wanted?** (DNA lists English as primary but Singapore is multilingual) | Language mix affects channel choice and creative production. |
