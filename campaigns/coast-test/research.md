## Executive Summary

- **The market is real, growing, and premiumising.** Singapore coffee market ~USD 31.9M (2025), forecast ~8.2% CAGR to ~USD 70.2M by 2035; home brewing is a named growth engine. [Source: Expert Market Research, 6Wresearch]
- **The competitive whitespace is clear.** Established SG roasters (PPP Coffee, Common Man, Hook, Bettr, Cowpresso, Perk, Tiong Hoe, Round Boy) compete largely on Latin American/African single origins. **None was found building a brand around multi-country Asian-origin curation.** [Source: direct competitor site visits — PPP Coffee, Hook Coffee, Bettr Coffee, Common Man, Tanamera, JJ Royal]
- **Three product differentiators form a unique combination not found together in any competitor:** (a) multi-country Asian-origin focus, (b) a named 3-tier adventurousness-based curation system, and (c) an explicit ≥6-week post-degassing freshness guarantee. [INFERENCE based on competitive comparison]
- **Asian-origin coffee is a strong rising trend.** A mosto-fermented coffee from Sirinya (Northern Thailand) was Climpson & Sons' top-selling filter coffee in 2025. Industry commentary expects Asian coffee interest to keep growing into 2026. Asia-Pacific is the fastest-growing coffee region. [Source: Climpson & Sons 2026 Trends, Mordor Intelligence]
- **Trio sampler at SGD 50–54 is competitively viable** — at ~SGD 16.67–18/bag in bundle, it undercuts the entry-level single-bag price of most competitors (SGD 17–28). [Source: competitor pricing from PPP Coffee SGD 17–28.50, Bettr Coffee SGD 19–79, Cowpresso SGD 17–25]
- **Target segment is reachable but small.** Estimated addressable home barista market in Singapore: ~15,000–30,000 households. 70 new customers = ~0.2–0.5% capture — achievable with sharp positioning. [INFERENCE based on Singapore population data + specialty coffee adoption estimates]

### 1.2 Competitive Whitespace Analysis

**What Coast Coffee has that no single competitor combines:**
1. **Multi-country Asian-origin focus** — Asian-origin specialists found (Tanamera, JJ Royal) are single-country (Indonesia) specialists; mainstream roasters carry Asian beans only occasionally (e.g. CMCR's Thailand lot, Bettr's Indonesia lots). **No competitor builds its brand on China + Thailand + Indonesia + Malaysia curation.**
2. **A named 3-tier curation system by adventurousness** — Common Man offers a quiz and PPP offers omakase, but **no competitor uses a fixed, legible "Tier 1 safe → Tier 2 complex-floral → Tier 3 exciting-unknown" framework** as the organising idea. This is Coast Coffee's most defensible differentiator.
3. **An explicit ≥6-week post-delivery freshness guarantee** — Competitors signal freshness via *roast recency* (Hook: "within 7 days of roasting"; Perk: "within 48 hours"). **None guarantees how long beans stay fresh after delivery.** Coast's vacuum-sealed + ≥6-week guarantee is structurally different and mail-order-specific.

[INFERENCE based on the FACTs above — no competitor was found combining all three.]

### 1.3 Indirect Competitors

- **Supermarket coffee brands** (Starbucks retail bags, generic supermarket beans) — SGD 8–15, commodity quality, no freshness guarantee, no curation.
- **International roasters shipping to SG** — higher shipping costs, longer transit, less freshness.
- **Local café bag sales** — require physical travel, contradicting DNA pain point "convenience of home delivery."

### 2.2 Asian-Origin Coffee: A Strong Rising Trend

**Direct evidence from the specialty coffee industry — Climpson & Sons (London roaster):**
> *"In 2025, a truly unique mosto-fermented coffee from Sirinya in Northern Thailand proved to be our top-selling filter coffee. It's one of those coffees that sticks in your memory — our most popular coffee the last two years! In 2026, we only expect to see interest in Asian coffees continue to grow."*
> *"Asia is set to generate most of the world's coffee demand growth over the next decade."*
[Source: Climpson & Sons, "2026 Specialty Coffee Trends to Watch" — climpsonandsons.com/blogs/journal/2026-specialty-coffee-trends-to-watch]

**Supporting data points:**
- **China (Yunnan):** Domestic coffee consumption grew 22% CAGR from 2006–2017; China doubled specialty green-bean imports 2020–2025. [Source: Wikipedia "Coffee production in China"; Climpson & Sons]
- **Thailand:** Thai Arabica quality improving significantly; Doi Tung and Doi Chang receiving EU protected designation of origin. [Source: Wikipedia "Coffee production in Thailand"]
- **Indonesia:** Bali, Java, Sumatra beans increasingly recognised in specialty circles (evidenced by Bettr Coffee offering "Bali Kala Patra" at SGD 24 and "Mt. Ijen" at SGD 19).
- **Singapore National Coffee Championship** 2025 drew 71 competitors across categories. Singapore Coffee Week is a recurring event. [Source: Time Out Singapore]

**Implication for Coast Coffee:** Asian-origin beans are not obscure — they are an **emerging premium category** with strong tailwinds. The "unfamiliar origins" objection can be reframed as "ahead of the curve."

### 2.4 Freshness: What Competitors Claim

| Competitor | Freshness Claim | Type of Guarantee |
|---|---|---|
| **Hook Coffee** | "Ships within 7 days of roasting" | Speed of delivery from roast |
| **Perk Coffee** | "Ships within 48 hours of roasting, roasts twice weekly" | Speed of delivery from roast |
| **Cowpresso** | "Freshly Roasting Daily," "100% Fresh Crop Arabica" | Daily roasting frequency |
| **PPP Coffee** | Small-batch roasting (implied freshness) | Batch size, not dated guarantee |
| **Common Man** | "Freshly roasted" positioning | General claim |
| **Coast Coffee** | **≥6 weeks after degassing + vacuum-sealing** | **Post-delivery shelf life guarantee** |

**Key insight:** All competitors claim freshness as *speed from roast to delivery*. None guarantees how long beans remain fresh *after arrival*. Coast Coffee's ≥6-week vacuum-sealed guarantee addresses a different axis — **lasting freshness vs. speed of delivery**. This is the most structurally distinct freshness claim in the market. [Source: direct competitor site observations + INFERENCE]

## 3. Audience Segmentation

### 3.1 Target Segment: Singapore Home Baristas

**Demographics (inferred from DNA + market observation):**
- Middle-to-upper income Singaporeans with disposable income for premium consumables
- Brew coffee at home as a ritual/hobby; own equipment (espresso machine, grinder, V60, Aeropress, French press)
- Seek quality, variety, discovery, and convenience
- Willing to pay SGD 18–30/bag for beans that deliver on freshness and flavour matching

**Psychographics (from DNA pain points + market research):**
- Want variety matched to preference (not guesswork)
- Want guaranteed freshness when ordering online
- Want home delivery convenience
- Want to discover interesting, less-common origins

**Shopping behaviour:** Research online; follow coffee communities/forums; read reviews; try subscription services or discovery samplers; active on Instagram for coffee content. [Source: Honeycombers/Best in Singapore listicle ecosystem confirms this audience reads editorial roundups]

These are **[INFERENCE]** segments constructed from observed market behaviour and the DNA's 3-tier flavour framework.

| Segment | "The Upgraders" (Gear-led) | "The Replenishers" (Convenience-seeking) | "The Explorers" (Flavour-adventurers) |
|---|---|---|---|
| **DNA tier match** | Tier 2–3 (complex-floral → exciting-unknown) | Tier 1 (safe & familiar, with upsell potential) | Tier 3 (exciting-unknown) |
| **JTBD** | "Help me brew something interesting and impressive at home and keep discovering." | "Keep good, reliable, fresh coffee arriving so my morning ritual never breaks." | "Surprise me with coffees I can't easily get and tell me what makes them special." |

## 4. First-Purchase Drivers & Objections

### 4.1 What Triggers a First Purchase

1. **Low-risk way to try before committing** — samplers/trial pricing are the category norm (PPP Surprise Box, Hook flexible subscription, Cowpresso from $17). [FACT: competitor observation]
2. **Removing the freshness gamble** of mail-order beans — the strongest concern for first-time DTC buyers. [DNA pain point]
3. **Novelty/discovery framed with guidance** — the 3-tier "pick by adventurousness" device reduces the risk of choosing wrong. [INFERENCE grounded in DNA differentiator #1]
4. **Clear value proposition vs. supermarket** — education on why specialty beans justify SGD 18–30 vs. SGD 8–15 commodity. [DNA inferred objection]

### 4.2 Objection Map

| Objection (from DNA) | Market Evidence | Coast Coffee's Available Answer |
|---|---|---|
| **"Will the beans be fresh on arrival?"** | Entire competitor set competes on freshness-speed claims — confirms this is the #1 DTC concern. [FACT] | ≥6-week post-degassing, vacuum-sealed guarantee + bean testing process. **State the guarantee explicitly; show the vacuum-seal/degassing process.** |
| **"I don't know these Asian origins — are they as good as origins I know?"** | Valid concern, BUT strong trend tailwind (Thailand/Yunnan rising; APAC fastest-growing). Unfamiliarity = opportunity. [FACT] | 3-tier framework lets nervous buyer start at Tier 1 (safe); pair each bag with clear flavour profile (as PPP/CMCR do). |
| **"Why pay SGD 18–30 vs. supermarket/ Hook ($14)?"** | Hook owns the low end at $14; Coast sits upper-middle of *specialty* pricing. The relevant comparison is other DTC specialty. [FACT] | Curation + guarantee + matched-to-taste justify premium. Trio frames cost as discovery value (3 experiences), not per-bag price. |
| **"Can I trust a new, small brand?"** | Hardest objection. Coast is unknown vs. established names (PPP "#1," CMCR café heritage). Testimonials listed as `<TO CONFIRM>` in DNA. [DNA gap] | Testing process + explicit guarantee (risk reversal) substitute for social proof initially. Collect first-buyer reviews during the 8-week run. |

## 6. Trio Offer Pricing Viability

### 6.1 Competitive Pricing Comparison

| Item | Price (SGD) | Source |
|---|---|---|
| Coast Coffee single bag (target) | 18–30 | DNA |
| Coast Coffee trio (proposed) | 50–54 | Goal (proposed) |
| Per-bag in trio bundle | ~16.67–18 | Implied |
| Hook Coffee single bag (lowest competitor) | From 14 | Hook Coffee |
| Cowpresso single bag | ~17–25 | Cowpresso |
| PPP Coffee single bag | 17–28.50 | PPP Coffee |
| Bettr Coffee single bag (standard) | 19–29 | Bettr Coffee |
| Bettr Coffee rare microlot | 65–79 | Bettr Coffee |
| Common Man single bag | 19.50–26 | Common Man |

**Finding:** At SGD 50–54 for three bags, Coast Coffee's trio sits at ~SGD 16.67–18/bag — competitive with Hook ($14/bag individually) and undercutting PPP ($17–28.50), Bettr ($19–29), and Common Man ($19.50–26). **However, the trio value must be justified by curation + discovery, not price alone** — price-led positioning would invite unfavourable comparison to Hook's $14 entry point. [INFERENCE]

### 6.2 Key Pricing Risk

If the trio's margin structure forces SGD 55–60+ (above the proposed SGD 50–54 range), the value message weakens — at SGD 60, per-bag cost is ~SGD 20, which is above Hook ($14) and at the top of Cowpresso ($17–25). **Customer must confirm trio economics (COGS, margin, shipping) before finalising.** [GAP flagged in intake §8.7]
