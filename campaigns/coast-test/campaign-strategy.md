## Executive Summary

- **Campaign approach:** A **discovery-first, low-commitment acquisition model** in three phases (Build → Launch → Amplify) across 8 weeks, with the 3-tier flavour trio as the primary conversion mechanic and the ≥6-week freshness guarantee as the primary risk-reversal.
- **Primary paid channel:** Instagram (paid) — visual discovery surface for Singapore coffee culture, best format for freshness/origin video storytelling. [RESEARCH §5.1]
- **Primary conversion channel:** Landing page (interactive 3-tier quiz + trio offer + freshness explainer) — optimised for CVR ≥ 3%. [STRATEGY handoff]
- **Organic leverage channel:** Editorial/listicle PR outreach — free, high-ROI, proven discovery path for SG home baristas. [RESEARCH #10]
- **Budget concentration rule:** With only SGD 1,000 total paid, concentrate on 2 channels (Instagram paid + Google Search) rather than spreading thin. Facebook retargeting added as a small third.
- **KPI ladder:** Creative KPIs (hook rate ≥ 25%, engagement rate ≥ 4%) → Marketing KPIs (CTR ≥ 1.0%, CVR ≥ 3%, CPP ≤ SGD 25) → Business KPI (70 new customers, blended CAC ≤ SGD 14, AOV ≥ SGD 50).
- **Key constraint honoured:** SGD 1,000 total paid across 8 weeks (~SGD 125/week). At this level, every dollar must earn its place — no experimental or low-conviction channel spend.

## 1. Campaign Approach

### 1.1 The Core Model: Discovery-First, Low-Commitment Acquisition

The campaign operates on a **three-phase model** that maps to the 8-week window and the buyer's journey:

| Phase | Weeks | Objective | Primary Channel(s) | Key Activity |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **1. Build (Weeks 1–2)** | 2 weeks | Awareness + education | Organic (Instagram carousels, editorial PR) + landing page pre-launch | Publish "Asian Coffee Atlas" educational content. Seed PR outreach to SG publishers (Honeycombers, Best in Singapore, TheSmartLocal, Time Out) for launch coverage. Build landing page with 3-tier quiz + trio offer. |
| **2. Launch (Weeks 3–5)** | 3 weeks | Conversion (paid + organic) | Instagram paid (lead video) + Google Search + landing page | Paid campaign goes live: lead freshness/origin video on Instagram (CPC ~SGD 0.75–1.00) + search ads on high-intent keywords. Trio offer live. Organic: community seeding (Reddit, Telegram/WhatsApp if verified). |
| **3. Amplify (Weeks 6–8)** | 3 weeks | Scale + retarget + harvest proof | Facebook retargeting + Instagram retargeting + email nurture | Retarget landing-page visitors who didn't purchase (Facebook/IG retargeting). Start email nurture sequence for first-time trio buyers (Curator Circle). Harvest reviews/UGC for social proof. |

**Why this approach (evidence trace):**
- **Discovery-first** matches the brand strategy's premise — the campaign's job is to let cold buyers *discover* the 3-tier system via a low-risk trio, not to hard-sell. [STRATEGY — campaign hypothesis]
- **Low-commitment** matches the category norm (competitors universally offer trial/low-commitment entry) [RESEARCH — "first-purchase triggers"] and the brand's position as an unknown vs. established names [RESEARCH #8].
- **Three phases** allow the tiny budget to build organic foundation first (weeks 1-2, zero spend), then concentrate paid spend in weeks 3-5 when the landing page and creative are optimised, then retarget in weeks 6-8 to capture warm leads.

### 1.2 Campaign Theme (Strategy-Level — For Creative to Execute)

The brand strategy's positioning (Asian-origin discovery roaster + 3-tier framework + freshness guarantee) should be expressed through a **single campaign organising idea** that Creative will concept around. From the strategy, the recommended organising idea is:

> **"Discover the Coast"** — a campaign about uncovering Asia's hidden coffee treasures, one bag at a time.

Why this theme fits (evidence trace):
- The brand name "Coast" + discovery theme creates a unified metaphor. [INFERENCE]
- DNA pain point: *"Want to discover interesting, less-common Asian-origin beans"* — discovery is the core customer job. [DNA]
- RESEARCH §4.1: *"Asian-origin beans are underexplored in the Singapore specialty market. Coast Coffee can own 'discover Asia through coffee' positioning."* [RESEARCH #1]
- The trio offer (one from each tier) maps naturally to a journey: "Start at safe shores (Tier 1), explore new straits (Tier 2), venture into open ocean (Tier 3)." [INFERENCE]

### 1.3 Offer Strategy

| Element | Detail | Evidence Trace |
|---|---|---|
| **Primary offer** | 3-bean flavour trio (one from each tier) at ~SGD 50–54 | [Goal; RESEARCH §6 — competitive viability] |
| **Framing** | "The whole system in one box" — discovery value, not discount | [STRATEGY — messaging: frame as curation, not price] |
| **Risk reversal** | ≥6-week freshness guarantee prominently stated at point of decision | [STRATEGY — pair trio with guarantee] |
| **Post-purchase** | Curator Circle — invite trio buyers to rate their bags, get matched to their next order | [STRATEGY — harvest reviews/UGC during run; build data for future subscription] |
| **Single-bag upsell** | After trio purchase, recommend a single bag from the tier they rated highest (free shipping incentive) | [INFERENCE — standard DTC upsell path; supports AOV ≥ SGD 50 secondary KPI] |

**Rationale for phasing:**
- **Weeks 1–2 (zero spend):** Build organic foundation first — publish Instagram carousels, seed PR outreach, finalise landing page. No point spending on ads before the landing page is live and optimised.
- **Weeks 3–5 (ramp up):** Launch paid when organic foundation + landing page are ready. Ramp spend as we learn which creative/audience combinations perform best.
- **Weeks 6–8 (retarget + optimise):** Shift toward retargeting as the paid audience pool grows. Reduce Instagram top-of-funnel spend; increase Facebook retargeting.
- **SGD 50 buffer:** Held in reserve for the best-performing channel in weeks 6-7.

**Implication for the business KPI:**
- Paid expected to drive ~28–63 conversions (midpoint ~40)
- Remaining 30–42 from organic (editorial PR, Instagram organic, word-of-mouth)
- **Blended total: ~70 ✓** — within range of the 70-customer target.
- **Implied blended CAC:** SGD 1,000 ÷ 70 = ~SGD 14.3 (close to ≤ SGD 14 target; within margin if paid drives 40+ or organic drives 30+).

> **⚠️ Risk:** If CVR on any channel underperforms (e.g. Instagram CVR drops to 2%), paid conversions could fall to ~18, putting pressure on organic to deliver 52 — ambitious for an unknown brand. **Mitigation:** The SGD 50 buffer + ability to reallocate underperforming channel budget mid-campaign.

The SGD 1,000 is **paid media spend only**. Creative production costs (video, photography, landing page build, copy) are assumed to be separate or in-house. If creative production must come from the same budget, the paid media allocation must be reduced — flagging this as an **open question for the customer**.

## 4. KPI Tiers — Complete Definition

### 4.1 KPI Ladder: How Creative → Marketing → Business KPIs Connect

```
Creative KPIs
  ↓ feed into
Marketing KPIs
  ↓ feed into
Business KPIs
```

The ladder ensures every creative decision traces to a marketing metric, which traces to the business objective:

**Creative → Marketing:** A strong hook rate (≥25%) drives higher CTR (≥1.0%) and lower CPC, which feeds into lower cost per purchase. High engagement rate (≥4%) on organic posts builds community and drives organic traffic to the landing page (CVR ≥ 3%).

**Marketing → Business:** CTR ≥ 1.0% + CVR ≥ 3% + CPP ≤ SGD 25 → at SGD 1,000 total paid, yields ~35–45 paid new customers. Add organic (~25–35) → 70 total. Blended CAC ≤ SGD 14. AOV ≥ SGD 50 via the trio offer.

### 4.2 KPI Tier Definitions

#### Business KPIs (Primary — What Matters to the Business)

| KPI | Target | How It's Measured | Why This Target | Evidence Trace |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **New customers (first-time buyers)** | **70 in 8 weeks** | E-commerce platform (Shopify/other) unique first-purchase count | Primary business objective from Goal. 70 customers in 8 weeks from a ~15K–30K addressable market = ~0.2–0.5% capture — achievable. | [Goal; RESEARCH §3.3: addressable market sizing] |
| **Blended CAC** | **≤ SGD 14** | Total cost (SGD 1,000 paid + organic costs) ÷ total new customers | Paid expected to drive ~35–45 conversions; organic drives remainder. CAC of SGD 14 implies 71 customers at SGD 1,000 total. | [Goal; RESEARCH §5.2: typical SG benchmarks] |
| **AOV (average order value)** | **≥ SGD 50** | Total revenue ÷ total orders | Trio offer at SGD 50–54 supports this directly. Single-bag orders (SGD 18–30) would drag AOV down — trio must be the primary first-purchase option. | [Goal — secondary KPI; RESEARCH §6 — trio pricing] |
| **Repeat purchase rate (secondary — weeks 9-12)** | **≥ 15% within 4 weeks** | % of first-time buyers who purchase again within 4 weeks | Indicates product-market fit and trio→subscription funnel potential. Not a primary KPI (8-week window may be tight) but important for LTV. | [INFERENCE — standard DTC retention benchmark; RESEARCH §4.4: "trio as acquisition funnel into subscription"] |

#### Marketing KPIs (Mid-Level — Channel and Conversion Performance)

| KPI | Target | How It's Measured | Why This Target | Evidence Trace |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Landing-page conversion rate** | **≥ 3%** | Sessions ÷ purchases on landing page | Competitive for DTC specialty goods in SG (benchmark 2–5%). The interactive 3-tier quiz module should lift CVR above baseline. | [Goal; RESEARCH §5.2: "Achievable with optimised landing page"] |
| **Cost per purchase (paid)** | **≤ SGD 25** | Total paid spend ÷ paid-attributed purchases | If CPP = SGD 25 and total paid spend = SGD 1,000, paid delivers 40 customers — right in the target zone. CPP > SGD 25 would require more organic to compensate. | [Goal; RESEARCH §5.2: "Achievable if CVR ≥ 3% and CPC ≤ SGD 0.75"] |
| **CTR on paid social** | **≥ 1.0%** | Clicks ÷ impressions (Instagram + Facebook ads) | SG benchmark 0.8–1.5%. The lead freshness/origin video (hook rate ≥ 25%) should support ≥1.0% CTR. | [Goal; RESEARCH §5.2] |
| **CPC on paid social** | **≤ SGD 1.00** | Total social spend ÷ clicks | SG benchmark SGD 0.50–1.50. Targeting narrow audience (SG home baristas, coffee interest) should keep CPC in lower half of range. | [RESEARCH §5.2] |
| **CTR on Google Search** | **≥ 3.0%** | Clicks ÷ impressions | Higher than social because search is intent-driven. Target "Asian coffee beans Singapore" and "specialty coffee delivery Singapore" as lower-competition keywords. | [INFERENCE — standard search benchmark for niche keywords] |
| **CPC on Google Search** | **≤ SGD 1.50** | Total search spend ÷ clicks | SG search CPC is moderate for specialty keywords. Niche keywords should be cheaper than generic "coffee Singapore." | [INFERENCE — SG search landscape] |

#### Creative KPIs (Foundation — Asset Performance)

| KPI | Target | How It's Measured | Which Asset | Why This Target | Evidence Trace |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Hook rate (3-sec / thumb-stop)** | **≥ 25%** | % of video impressions where viewer stays ≥ 3 seconds | Lead freshness/origin video (Instagram paid) | The lead video is the primary awareness asset. Hook rate ≥ 25% means 1 in 4 viewers stops scrolling — strong enough to feed the paid funnel at scale. | [Goal; Creative KPI tied to paid social CTR] |
| **Engagement rate on organic posts** | **≥ 4%** | (Likes + comments + saves + shares) ÷ impressions | Organic Instagram posts (carousels, single images, Stories) | SG coffee content typically sees 3–6% engagement. ≥ 4% indicates content resonance and community building. | [Goal; RESEARCH — Honeycombers/Best in SG listicle ecosystem confirms high engagement for coffee content] |
| **Save rate on educational carousels** | **≥ 5%** | Saves ÷ impressions | "Asian Coffee Atlas" carousel (Concept 1 — educational) | Educational content that gets saved signals value (people want to return to it). High save rate feeds Instagram's algorithm for organic reach. | [INFERENCE — Instagram best practice; educational carousels for niche interests typically see 3–8% save rates] |
| **Video completion rate (15+ sec)** | **≥ 15%** | % of viewers who watch ≥ 15 seconds | Freshness explainer video (Instagram — short form) | Indicates whether the freshness guarantee message is compelling enough to hold attention beyond the hook. | [INFERENCE — video benchmark for 15-sec content in DTC] |

### 4.3 KPI Tracking Cadence

| KPI | Review Frequency | Owner | Decision if Below Target |
|---|---|---|---|
| **New customers (weekly)** | Weekly (every Monday) | Marketing Director | Investigate channel underperformance; reallocate budget from worst channel to best |
| **Blended CAC** | Weekly | Marketing Director | If CAC > SGD 14 after week 4, reduce paid spend and increase organic/PR effort |
| **Landing-page CVR** | Daily (first 2 weeks), then weekly | Marketing Director / Performance | If CVR < 3%, A/B test landing page: 3-tier quiz placement, trio price visibility, freshness guarantee prominence |

| **Engagement rate** | Weekly (organic) | Content Manager / Creative Director | If engagement < 4%, adjust content mix: more educational carousels, more UGC, more interactive elements (polls, questions) |

## 6. Key Risks & Mitigations

| Risk | Impact | Likelihood | Mitigation |
|---|---|---|---|
| **CVR below 3% on landing page** | Paid conversions drop from ~35 to ~23; blended CAC rises to ~SGD 19 | Medium | A/B test: move 3-tier quiz above the fold, add freshness guarantee badge near CTA button, test trio price visibility |
| **Hook rate below 25% on lead video** | CTR drops → CPC rises → CPP rises above SGD 25 | Medium | Re-edit video with different hooks — test freshness (ASMR vacuum-seal) vs. curiosity ("where does your coffee come from?") vs. adventurousness ("how adventurous are you?") |
| **Organic (editorial PR) delivers 0 customers** | Paid must deliver all 70, requiring CPP ≤ SGD 14.3 — unrealistic | Low-Medium | Pursue PR aggressively in Build phase (weeks 1-2). If no coverage by week 3, redirect organic effort to Instagram community building |
| **Trio economics force price above SGD 54** | Value message weakens; AOV target (≥ SGD 50) harder to hit | Unknown (customer must confirm) | If price must rise, adjust AOV target. Do not launch with trio price above SGD 60 without revisiting positioning |
| **Subscription expectation causes bounce** | Cold buyers expect subscription option (standard in market) and leave if absent | Medium | On landing page, add "Prefer a regular delivery? Join the waitlist for our upcoming subscription" — captures emails and sets expectation |
| **No testimonials → trust objection kills conversion** | CVR below 2% as cold buyers don't trust an unknown brand | High (given DNA gap) | Front-load the guarantee + testing process on every page. Collect first-buyer reviews aggressively from week 3 onward. Consider a "first 50 customers get a handwritten thank-you note" to encourage organic sharing |

## 8. Handoff to Creative Direction
