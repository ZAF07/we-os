## Executive Summary

- **Positioning (one line):** Coast Coffee is the **Asian-origin discovery roaster for Singapore home baristas** — the one that organises beans by how adventurous you feel and guarantees they stay fresh for weeks.
- **The 3-tier flavour framework is the spine of the whole strategy.** It is simultaneously the positioning idea, the objection-handler ("start safe"), the value-prop pillar, and the structure of the trio offer. [DNA #1; RESEARCH #2]
- **The defensible position is the *combination* of three things no competitor unites:** (a) multi-country Asian-origin focus, (b) the 3-tier adventurousness system, and (c) the ≥6-week freshness guarantee. [RESEARCH — whitespace analysis] Coast must own all three together; any one alone is copyable.
- **Hardest problem is trust, not interest.** No testimonials on file [DNA gap; RESEARCH #8]. Strategy compensates via risk-reversal (the guarantee) + process transparency (testing/degassing) as substitutes for social proof until first-buyer reviews are harvested during the 8-week run.
- **Premium-justified, never price-led.** Hook owns the low end at SGD 14 [RESEARCH #5]. Coast's messaging must justify SGD 18–30 through curation + guarantee + matched-to-taste, and frame the trio as *discovery value*, never a discount.
- **Recommended voice (to validate):** the **knowledgeable guide** — expert but not gatekeeping, warm, plain-spoken, confident on the guarantee, curious about origins. [REC — DNA lists voice as `<TO CONFIRM>`]

## 1. Positioning Statement

> **For** Singapore home baristas who want to brew distinctive coffee at home and keep discovering new beans,
> **who are** wary of gambling on unfamiliar origins or receiving stale mail-order beans,
> **Coast Coffee** is the **Asian-origin specialty coffee service**
> **that** lets them explore beans from across Asia — matched to exactly how adventurous they feel — with freshness they can count on,
> **because** every bag is sorted into a clear 3-tier framework (safe → complex → exciting), tested before it ships, and **guaranteed fresh for at least 6 weeks** after degassing and vacuum-sealing.

### Why this positioning (evidence trace)

**Category we choose to compete in:** *Asian-origin discovery roaster / curated specialty-bean service.* Deliberately **not** "another SG specialty roaster" (a crowded field led by PPP and Common Man on global origins) and **not** a single-country heritage play (Tanamera/JJ Royal own Indonesia-as-heritage). [RESEARCH #1 — whitespace]

**Why this claim is defensible (evidence trace):**
- No SG competitor positions around **multi-country Asian-origin curation** as a core brand identity. [RESEARCH #1 — direct site visits to 10 competitors]
- No competitor uses a **fixed, legible "Tier 1 safe → Tier 3 unknown" framework** as the organising idea. Common Man has a quiz, PPP has omakase — neither names and structures by adventurousness. [RESEARCH #2]
- No competitor **guarantees post-delivery freshness** — they compete on speed from roast to delivery (Hook: "within 7 days," Perk: "within 48 hours"). Coast's ≥6-week vacuum-sealed guarantee addresses a structurally different axis. [RESEARCH #3]

**Why this positioning serves the business objective (70 new customers in 8 weeks, CAC ≤ SGD 14):**
- A **distinct claim** is what earns the click and the first purchase from a cold audience. A "me-too specialty roaster" claim would lose to brands with more authority and budget. [INFERENCE]
- The "**who are** wary…" clause names the two first-purchase frictions research validated (freshness-on-arrival, unfamiliar origins) [RESEARCH — objection map] and makes the positioning *answer the objection inside the claim itself*, lowering perceived risk and directly serving CAC.
- It reframes Coast's biggest apparent weakness (unfamiliar origins) into the **reason to buy** (discovery), riding the documented Asian-coffee trend tailwind (Climpson & Sons: Thai mosto-fermented was top-selling filter coffee in 2025; APAC fastest-growing region). [RESEARCH #4]

### What we deliberately choose NOT to claim

| Claim | Who owns it | Why we cede it | Evidence |
|---|---|---|---|
| "Cheapest coffee" / best value | Hook Coffee (from SGD 14) | Competing on price would invite unfavourable comparison and devalue the curation positioning. Hook owns this space. | [RESEARCH #5] |
| "Singapore's #1 roaster" / biggest | PPP Coffee (established 2009, 3 stores + omakase) | We can't win on scale or tenure; we shouldn't try. | [RESEARCH — competitor landscape] |
| "Café heritage" / in-person experience | Common Man Coffee Roasters (café chain + academy) | Coast is DTC-first; café heritage is irrelevant to our model. | [RESEARCH] |
| "Social mission / sustainability" | Bettr Coffee (registered social enterprise) | DNA doesn't list sustainability as a core differentiator — adopting it as a claim would be fabricated. | [DNA — no sustainability differentiator listed] |
| "Single-country provenance / heritage" | Tanamera, JJ Royal (Indonesia) | Coast's differentiator is *multi-country* Asian curation, not any single origin. | [RESEARCH #1 — competitive gap] |

## 2. Value Proposition

### Primary value proposition (strategy statement — not a finished tagline)

> **"Adventurous Asian coffee, picked for your taste — and guaranteed to arrive fresh."**

This carries all three whitespace elements (Asian + matched-to-taste curation + freshness guarantee) and answers the overarching customer job: **"Let me discover interesting coffee at home without gambling on taste or freshness."** [DNA pain points; RESEARCH JTBD]

### Supporting pillars

Each pillar traces to a DNA differentiator and a customer job-to-be-done (JTBD), and is ranked by importance for a **cold first-time buyer** (the 70-new-customer objective):

| Pillar | DNA Differentiator | Customer JTBD | Why It Matters for Acquiring 70 New Customers | Evidence Trace |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **1. Pick by how adventurous you feel (the 3 tiers)** — *safe → complex-floral → exciting-unknown* | DNA #1: 3-tier flavour framework matched to adventurousness | *"Match me to coffee I'll actually like — let me start safe and explore at my own pace."* | **Converts the cold/nervous majority.** The legible "start at Tier 1" on-ramp removes taste-risk, the #1 reason a first-timer hesitates. Also makes the trio self-explanatory. | [DNA #1] [RESEARCH #2: "3-tier framework is unique"] [RESEARCH — objection map: unfamiliarity concern] |
| **2. Fresh when it lands — guaranteed ≥6 weeks** | DNA #2: Tested, vacuum-sealed after degassing, ≥6-week freshness guarantee | *"Don't make me gamble on stale mail-order beans."* | **The most differentiated, hardest-to-copy claim.** Competitors promise only roast recency, not a guarantee. This is the campaign's primary risk-reversal lever for a brand with no reviews yet. | [DNA #2] [RESEARCH #3: "structurally different freshness claim"] [RESEARCH #8: trust weakest link] |
| **3. Coffee from across Asia you can't easily get** | DNA #3: Asian-origin focus (China, Thailand, Indonesia, Malaysia) | *"Show me coffees I haven't tried and tell me what makes them special."* | **The novelty hook that earns attention** from flavour-adventurers and rides the Asian-coffee trend. Distinct from Indonesia-only heritage players. | [DNA #3] [RESEARCH #4: Asian-origin trend] [RESEARCH #1: competitive whitespace] |
| **4. Brought to your door, matched to you** | DNA #4 (home delivery) + DNA #5 (matched to individual profiles) | *"Keep good coffee arriving so my ritual never breaks — without me travelling to buy."* | **Serves the "replenisher" cohort** and the convenience-led DTC norm. Underwrites repeat/AOV after the first trio purchase. | [DNA #4, #5] [RESEARCH — "The Replenishers" segment] |

**Ranking for a cold launch** [REC]: Lead with **Pillar 1 (curation)** to earn interest and remove taste-risk. Prove with **Pillar 2 (guarantee)** to dissolve freshness concerns. Differentiate with **Pillar 3 (Asian novelty)** to stand out. Pillar 4 is table-stakes convenience for retention.

### Why this structure

Pillars 1–3 *are* the three whitespace elements — they make the value prop **defensible**, not generic. Pillar 4 is the table-stakes convenience layer. Each pillar maps to a real DNA differentiator and a stated/inferred customer job, satisfying the "tie every claim to DNA + a why" rule.

### 3.3 How Messaging Maps to the 3 Flavour Tiers

The tiers are a **messaging device**, not just a SKU list — each speaks to a different mindset and behavioural sub-segment [RESEARCH — three segments]:

| Tier | Message Angle | Speaks To | Why It Matters |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Tier 1 — safe & basic** | *"A reliable, crowd-pleasing cup to start with."* | Replenishers / first-timers nervous about taste | Gives the cautious buyer a no-regret entry point — the on-ramp that converts. [RESEARCH — "The Replenishers"] |

### 3.4 How Messaging Maps to the Trio Sampler Offer

- **Frame the trio as "the whole system in one box," not a discount.** Its job is to let a cold buyer experience all three tiers in a single low-risk purchase — the category-validated low-commitment entry. [RESEARCH — "Low-risk sampler is category norm"]
- **Discovery value, not price value.** Messaging emphasises *three distinct experiences, a guided journey from safe to bold* — which justifies the ~SGD 50–54 price and protects the premium position. [RESEARCH #5: "pricing is premium-justified, not price-led"; RESEARCH — "trio value must be justified by curation + discovery, not price alone"]
- **Pair the trio with the guarantee at the point of decision.** The headline offer and the headline risk-reversal should arrive together, maximising first-purchase conversion toward the 70-customer goal.

### 3.5 Objection-Handling Messages

## 4. Brand Personality & Voice (RECOMMENDATION — DNA marks voice as `<TO CONFIRM>`)

### 4.1 Recommended Personality: The Knowledgeable Guide

An expert friend who has tasted everything, has strong opinions, and wants *you* to discover what you'll love. Confident enough to stand behind a freshness guarantee; humble enough to let you start safe.

**Why:** The audience is sophisticated and discovery-minded (upgraders and flavour-adventurers who value transparency, provenance, and brew knowledge) [RESEARCH — segments]. They respond to credible expertise, not hype — and for an unknown brand, *demonstrated expertise is itself a trust signal* that partly compensates for the missing testimonials. A guide persona also makes the 3-tier framework feel like helpful curation rather than gatekeeping.

### 4.2 Voice Traits

1. **Knowledgeable, not gatekeeping** — explain origins and flavour like a friend who happens to be an expert; never make the reader feel they should already know.
2. **Warm and plain-spoken** — accessible English; specialty terms are introduced, not assumed.
3. **Confident about the guarantee** — direct and unhedged on freshness; this is where the brand is allowed to be boldest.
4. **Curious and discovery-led** — genuinely excited about lesser-known Asian beans; invites exploration.
5. **Honest and specific** — concrete details (origin, process, flavour notes) over superlatives; never overstate the ≥6-week promise (a DNA hard constraint). [DNA]

### 4.3 Do's and Don'ts

| Do | Don't |
|---|---|
| Speak plainly; explain flavour and origin in everyday terms | Use coffee jargon as a barrier or status flex |
| Be specific (country, process, tasting notes) | Lean on vague superlatives ("the best coffee ever") |
| State the freshness guarantee directly and confidently | Overstate it beyond ≥6 weeks, or hedge it into meaninglessness [DNA constraint] |
| Invite the nervous buyer to "start safe" | Shame or pressure ("real coffee people drink it black") |
| Frame Asian origins as exciting and ahead-of-trend | Frame them as cheap, exotic, or a novelty gimmick |
| Justify price through value (curation, testing, matching) | Apologise for price or compete on being cheap |

### 4.4 Illustrative Phrasings (Tone Only — NOT Approved Copy)

> *"New to these origins? Start with Tier 1 — easy, reliable, and a great first cup. Ready for something stranger? Tier 3 is waiting."*
>
> *"We taste every lot before it ships, vacuum-seal it right after degassing, and guarantee it's fresh for at least six weeks. If it's not, that's on us."*
>
> *"Yunnan, Northern Thailand, the Indonesian archipelago — the coffees most roasters skip. Here's why we think they're worth your morning."*

## 6. Campaign Hypothesis (The Bet This Campaign Makes)

### The Hypothesis

> **If** we communicate Coast Coffee's three-part differentiation (Asian-origin curation + 3-tier flavour system + ≥6-week freshness guarantee) through a low-risk trio sampler offer that lets home baristas discover their preference tier,
> **then** we will acquire 70 new first-time buyers in 8 weeks at a blended CAC ≤ SGD 14,
> **because** the combination (a) occupies a genuine competitive whitespace no incumbent owns, (b) directly answers the two biggest first-purchase objections (taste-risk and freshness gamble), and (c) rides a documented rising trend (Asian-origin coffee) that makes "unfamiliar" feel "ahead of the curve" rather than risky.

### Why This Hypothesis Is the Right Bet (Evidence Trace)

1. **Whitespace is real and defensible.** Research confirmed no major SG competitor combines multi-country Asian origin + 3-tier adventurousness system + post-delivery freshness guarantee. [RESEARCH #1, #2, #3]
2. **The trio sampler is a category-validated acquisition mechanic.** Competitors universally offer low-commitment entries (PPP Surprise Box, Hook flexible subscription, Cowpresso from $17). [RESEARCH — "First-purchase triggers"]
3. **The freshness guarantee is the strongest risk-reversal.** For a brand with no testimonials, the ≥6-week guarantee (structurally different from competitors' roast-recently claims) is the primary trust signal. [RESEARCH #3, #8]
4. **Asian-origin trend provides external credibility.** Top-selling filter coffee globally was a Thai mosto-fermented lot in 2025; APAC is the fastest-growing coffee region. [RESEARCH #4] This trend tailwind makes the positioning timely, not obscure.
5. **The 3-tier framework is self-explanatory and objection-proof.** It lets buyers start safe, explore at their own pace, and understand the trio without complex explanation. [RESEARCH #2; DNA #1]

### Risks to Monitor

| Risk | Why It's a Risk | Mitigation |
|---|---|---|
| **Trust gap** — no testimonials, Coast is unknown vs. established brands | First-time buyers may hesitate without social proof. [RESEARCH #8] | Lead with guarantee + testing process as risk reversal; actively harvest first-buyer reviews during the run. |
| **Trio economics unconfirmed** — if margin forces SGD 55–60+, value message weakens | At SGD 60 (~SGD 20/bag), per-bag cost rivals PPP/Bettr mid-range and loses the competitive pricing edge. [RESEARCH — pricing risk] | Customer must confirm economics before finalising offer price. If margin is tight, reduce the trio to 2 bags or maintain at SGD 54 max. |
| **Asian-origin education cost** — unfamiliar origins require more explanation | A cold audience may not know Yunnan, Doi Tung, or Bali Kintamani. This increases copy length and creative burden. [RESEARCH #4: "unfamiliar origins" objection] | The 3-tier framework reduces education need — start at Tier 1 (safe/familiar), explore later. Use flavour notes, not geography, as primary descriptors. |
| **Freshness claim (≥6 weeks) may be compared unfavourably to Hook's "within 7 days of roasting"** | A buyer who values peak freshness may prefer Hook's speed-of-delivery model over Coast's shelf-life model. [RESEARCH #3] | Position as *different axis* (lasting freshness vs. speed of delivery), not better/worse. Coast's guarantee is about *flexibility* — beans that stay fresh even if you don't finish the bag immediately. |
| **Hook's SGD 14 entry point is a permanent price anchor** | Any trio price above ~SGD 42 (3 × SGD 14) invites "isn't this more expensive than Hook?" comparisons. [RESEARCH #5] | Don't compete on per-bag price vs. Hook. Frame trio as *curated experience* (guided journey through 3 tiers) not 3 individual bags. Justify premium via curation + guarantee. |
| **Subscription expectation** — competitors all offer subscriptions; Coast doesn't appear to have one yet | First-time buyers may expect a subscription option and bounce if it's absent. [RESEARCH #9] | The trio can function as an acquisition funnel into a future subscription. Flag to customer that subscription is the standard expectation. |

## 7. Handoff Notes to Downstream Stages

### What the Campaign Strategy Stage Must Honour

1. **Lead with curation, prove with guarantee, differentiate with Asian origin.** The hero idea is the *combination* — not any single feature.
2. **The 3-tier framework is the central organising idea** — it should structure the campaign approach, the landing page flow, and the offer presentation.
3. **Pair the trio offer with the freshness guarantee at the point of decision** — headline offer + headline risk-reversal together, in service of first-purchase conversion.
4. **Trust is the weakest link and there are no testimonials yet.** Campaign tactics must include a mechanism to **capture first-buyer reviews/UGC during the 8-week run** so social proof compounds for later phases.
5. **Premium, never cheap.** Do not signal discount or compete on price; justify pricing through curation, testing, the guarantee, and matched-to-taste.
6. **Reframe "unfamiliar Asian origins" as "ahead of the curve,"** using the trend tailwind — never as cheap/exotic novelty.
7. **Voice = the knowledgeable guide** (knowledgeable-not-gatekeeping, warm, plain-spoken, confident on the guarantee, honest/specific) — **pending customer validation** (DNA voice is `<TO CONFIRM>`).
8. **Stay inside DNA constraints:** at most 3 flavour variations, SGD 18–30/bag band, ≥6-week freshness promise not overstated, English-primary.
