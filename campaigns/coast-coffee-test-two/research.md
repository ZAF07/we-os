## Executive summary

- **The market picture has not changed materially from the prior research (June 2026).** Singapore coffee market ~USD 31.9M (2025), ~8.2% CAGR to 2035, with home brewing as a named growth driver. The Asian-origin coffee trend (Thailand, Yunnan/China, APAC fastest-growing region) remains a tailwind. **[PRIOR-RESEARCH FACT]**
- **The competitive whitespace is unchanged.** No SG competitor was found in the prior research combining **(a) multi-country Asian-origin focus, (b) a 3-tier curation system by adventurousness, and (c) an explicit ≥6-week freshness guarantee.** Coast's defensibility holds — but it is contingent on the guarantee and curation being real and provable, not just claimed. **[PRIOR-RESEARCH INFERENCE]**
- **This campaign's higher target — 70 new customers vs. the prior 50 — raises the bar on conversion efficiency by ~40% at the same SGD 1,000 paid budget.** The prior campaign strategy expected paid to drive ~35–45 of 50; this campaign needs paid to drive ~35–45 of 70, requiring **organic/earned/community channels to contribute 25–35 customers** (vs. ~5–15 before). The research therefore places more weight on understanding organic-reach channels and first-purchase triggers that don't rely on paid spend. **[THIS-CAMPAIGN]**
- **Three behavioural sub-segments remain valid** — gear-led enthusiasts, convenience-seeking replenishers, and flavour-adventurers — and the flavour-adventurer remains the sharpest fit for an Asian-origin discovery sampler. **[PRIOR-RESEARCH INFERENCE; re-validated against DNA]**
- **Trust-in-a-new-brand remains the hardest objection** with no testimonials yet on file. For this higher-target run, the campaign's ability to **harvest and deploy social proof during the 8 weeks** (from early buyers) is even more critical than in the prior campaign, because organic acquisition — which depends on word-of-mouth and credibility signals — must carry a larger share of the 70-customer goal. **[THIS-CAMPAIGN INFERENCE]**
- **The trio sampler at SGD 50–54 is the right acquisition vehicle** — category-validated, maps to all three tiers, and the prior research's competitor analysis confirms no equivalent "3-tier discovery box" exists. The question is whether the economics work at that price (flagged as "confirm economics" in the goal — see gaps). **[PRIOR-RESEARCH FACT + INFERENCE]**

## 1. Target audience / segmentation
*Method: behavioural audience segmentation + JTBD. Builds on prior research segments; re-validated against the same Customer DNA.*

### Who SG home baristas are (unchanged context)
- Singapore has a mature specialty coffee culture and an active home-brewing movement driven by remote/hybrid work, lifestyle upgrades, and rising quality appreciation. Consumers invest in grinders, scales, pour-over kits, frothers, and home roasters. **[PRIOR-RESEARCH FACT]**
- Specialty adoption is broadening beyond expat circles, with local consumers showing growing sophistication in single-origin profiles and brewing technique. **[PRIOR-RESEARCH FACT]**
- Enthusiast community hubs include the **Singapore National Coffee Championship** (71 competitors across disciplines in 2025), community roasters like **Homeground Coffee Roasters** (explicitly building a "community of home brewers"), **Nylon Coffee Roasters**, **Fluid Collective**, and a documented **home-café movement** (HDB-based). **[PRIOR-RESEARCH FACT]**

### Three behavioural sub-segments (re-validated — mapped to DNA tiers)
*Segments are **[INFERENCE]** constructed from behavioural patterns and aligned to the DNA's 3-tier framework. Sizing remains unquantified (see gaps).*

1. **Gear-led enthusiasts ("the upgraders").** Own/are buying grinders, scales, pour-over kits. Value provenance, transparency (process, varietal, roast date), and brew control. Lower price sensitivity for distinctive lots; high knowledge. Hang out around community roasters, competitions, brew-method content. *Maps to Tier 2/3 (complex-floral → exciting-unknown).* JTBD: *"Help me brew something interesting and impressive at home and keep discovering."* **[PRIOR-RESEARCH INFERENCE]**
2. **Convenience-seeking subscribers ("the replenishers").** Brew daily, value not running out and not having to travel to buy; the DNA's explicit "prefer beans delivered" cohort. Moderate price sensitivity; loyal once a routine forms. Reachable via subscription/DTC channels and search. *Maps to Tier 1 (basic & safe) entry point, with upsell potential.* JTBD: *"Keep good, reliable, fresh coffee arriving so my morning ritual never breaks."* **[PRIOR-RESEARCH INFERENCE; grounded in DNA pain point "convenience of home delivery"]**
3. **Flavour-adventurers ("the explorers").** Actively seek novel origins, processes, and "something I haven't tried." Lower price sensitivity *for novelty*, higher for repeat staples. Most responsive to "exciting & unknown" Asian origins. *Maps directly to Tier 3, and to the trio sampler as a whole.* JTBD: *"Surprise me with coffees I can't easily get and tell me what makes them special."* **[PRIOR-RESEARCH INFERENCE]**

### Segment implications for the 70-customer target [THIS-CAMPAIGN]
- The prior campaign likely targeted primarily **flavour-adventurers + gear-led enthusiasts** — the highest-intent, most discovery-receptive segments reachable via paid social/search. To hit 70 (vs. 50), the campaign must also **convert replenishers**, who are less novelty-driven and more routine/loyalty-oriented.
- **Replenishers are harder to acquire via paid discovery** — they convert through search intent ("coffee bean delivery Singapore") and word-of-mouth/referral, not through novelty-driven social ads. This reinforces the need for organic/earned channels to carry weight.
- The trio sampler appeals to all three segments but for different reasons: explorers want the novelty, upgraders want the tasting flight, and replenishers want a low-risk way to find their go-to bag (likely Tier 1). Messaging should flex by segment — a single "discovery" frame may under-serve replenishers.

### What triggers a first purchase (audience-level — unchanged)
- A **low-risk way to try** before committing (sampler/trial pricing) — category norm. **[PRIOR-RESEARCH FACT]**
- **Removing the freshness gamble** of mail-order beans. **[DNA pain point; PRIOR-RESEARCH INFERENCE]**
- **Novelty/discovery** framed with enough guidance not to feel like a gamble of taste — the 3-tier "pick by adventurousness" device directly answers this. **[PRIOR-RESEARCH INFERENCE]**
- **New for this campaign:** the need to trigger first purchase *without paid ad exposure* for a larger share of the target — via search, editorial, community, and referral — places more weight on **search-optimised landing pages, editorial inclusion, and community credibility.** **[THIS-CAMPAIGN INFERENCE]**

### Whitespace for Coast Coffee (re-validated — unchanged) [PRIOR-RESEARCH INFERENCE]
1. **Multi-country Asian-origin curation.** Asian-origin players (Tanamera, JJ Royal) are **single-country Indonesia specialists**; mainstream roasters lead with Latin American/African origins and carry Asian beans only occasionally (e.g. CMCR's Thailand lot). No competitor was found building its brand on a **China + Thailand + Indonesia + Malaysia** spread.
2. **An explicit freshness *guarantee*.** Competitors signal freshness via *roast recency* ("48 hours / 7 days from roast," "roasting daily"). Coast's **≥6-week post-degassing, vacuum-sealed guarantee** is a structurally different, mail-order-specific promise — no equivalent found.
3. **A named curation system tied to adventurousness.** CMCR offers a quiz and PPP an omakase, but **no competitor offers a fixed, legible "Tier 1 safe → Tier 3 unknown" framework** as the organising idea. This is Coast's most defensible differentiator.
4. **Pricing context:** Coast's SGD 18–30 band sits at the **upper-middle** of the market (Hook from $14; PPP/CMCR/Cowpresso ~$17–28.50). The trio at ~SGD 50–54 is competitive vs. buying three single bags but is **not** an undercut play — it must be justified by curation + guarantee, not price. **[PRIOR-RESEARCH INFERENCE]**

*Method: channel/community mapping. Prior channel findings reproduced; new analysis on organic-reach requirements for the 70-customer target.*

### Where this audience is reachable in Singapore (unchanged from prior research)

**Search (Google):** Strong commercial-intent behaviour exists — ranked listicles ("best coffee roasters/beans Singapore," "coffee subscription Singapore," "buy coffee beans online Singapore") are abundant and competitive, indicating people actively search to buy/compare. SEO + paid search are live channels; specialty roasters compete here. **[PRIOR-RESEARCH FACT]**

**Editorial / listicle ecosystem:** High-traffic SG publishers shape discovery — **The Honeycombers, Best in Singapore, TheSmartLocal, Time Out Singapore, SETHLUI, DanielFoodDiary, Quality Life Coffee**. Inclusion in "best roasters / best coffee subscriptions" roundups is a recurring acquisition path for competitors (Hook, PPP all appear). **[PRIOR-RESEARCH FACT]**

**Community & events (high-intent enthusiasts):** **Singapore Coffee Association / National Coffee Championship**, **Singapore Coffee Week**, and community roasters that convene home brewers — **Homeground (Joo Chiat), Nylon (Everton Park), Fluid Collective (Potong Pasir)**. The **home-café movement** is an emerging cultural pocket. **[PRIOR-RESEARCH FACT]**

**Social:** Instagram is the dominant visual/discovery surface for SG café & coffee culture. **[PRIOR-RESEARCH FACT]** TikTok and Facebook are **plausible but not confirmed** for this niche. Reddit/Telegram presence is **plausible but unverified.** **[PRIOR-RESEARCH GAPS]**

### Channel implications for the 70-customer target [THIS-CAMPAIGN]
The prior campaign strategy allocated paid as ~SGD 1,000 split across Google Search (~35%), Meta/IG (~45%), and a retargeting reserve (~20%). Organic/earned was budgeted at SGD 0 (effort only). That allocation expected paid to drive ~35–45 of 50 customers.

**For 70 customers at the same SGD 1,000 budget:**
- Paid likely still drives ~35–45 customers (same budget, same economics).
- The remaining **25–35 customers must come from organic/earned/referral/community** — roughly 3×–5× what the prior campaign needed from non-paid channels.
- This means **editorial inclusion, community credibility, and word-of-mouth are not nice-to-haves — they are mathematically necessary.** The campaign should prioritise:
  - **Pitching to at least 3–5 listicle/editorial outlets** (Honeycombers, TheSmartLocal, SETHLUI, Time Out, Best in Singapore) — inclusion in even one or two can drive meaningful traffic.
  - **Engaging SG coffee communities directly** (Homeground, Nylon, Fluid Collective, home-café movement) — not as paid placements, but as genuine community participation that earns credibility and referrals.
  - **Designing a referral/share mechanism into the trio purchase experience** — e.g. a post-purchase "share your trio tasting" prompt — to convert early buyers into acquisition channels.
  - **SEO/content investment** — capturing search volume for Asian-origin and specialty coffee delivery queries, which compounds over the 8 weeks without ongoing paid spend. **[THIS-CAMPAIGN INFERENCE]**

## Implications for positioning (handoff notes for the Brand Strategy Agent — NOT positioning itself)
*Observations the next stage should weigh; deliberately not writing the positioning, value prop, or messaging.*

- **The whitespace is unchanged and still defensible** — Asian-origin + 3-tier curation + ≥6-week guarantee. The prior brand strategy's positioning ("the Asian-origin discovery roaster for Singapore home baristas") remains valid. Brand Strategy should review and adapt, not reinvent.
- **This campaign needs the positioning to work harder on trust and on replenisher conversion.** The prior positioning leaned toward discovery/adventure (flavour-adventurers). To hit 70 customers, the positioning must also speak convincingly to the cautious first-timer ("start safe at Tier 1") and the convenience-seeker ("find your go-to bag, then we'll keep it coming").
- **The trio sampler is not just the hero offer — it's the trust engine.** For a cold audience asked to try a new brand, the trio's structure (3 tiers, 3 experiences, one low-risk purchase) *is* the risk-reversal. Positioning should make the trio feel like the obvious, safe choice — not a gamble on three bags.
- **Editorial and community channels are not downstream afterthoughts.** The brand strategy should consider what story is pitchable to Honeycombers/TheSmartLocal/SETHLUI — the "Asian-origin discovery" angle is inherently media-friendly (it's a genuine trend story, not a manufactured one). Positioning that's easy for editors to retell earns free acquisition.
- **Pricing is premium-justified, never price-led** — unchanged. But for this higher-target run, the trio's ~SGD 50–54 must feel like *undeniable value*, not just a fair price. The framing matters more because more sceptics must convert.
- **Social proof is a dependency, not a given.** Strategy should bake in a plan to harvest first-buyer reviews and deploy them publicly by mid-campaign; positioning without proof remains thin against the "trust a new brand" objection.
