# Campaign Strategy — Coast Coffee Test Two (70-Customer Acquisition)

> Author: Marketing Director. Output is **campaign strategy only** — approach, channels-at-a-glance, budget allocation, and KPI tiers. No creative concepts, no asset briefs, no channel-level execution (those are downstream).
>
> Grounded in `customers/coast-coffee/dna.md` (Customer DNA), `campaigns/coast-coffee-test-two/goal.md` (campaign goal), `campaigns/coast-coffee-test-two/brand-strategy.md` (approved brand strategy), and `campaigns/coast-coffee-test-two/research.md` (research findings).
>
> **Binding constraints from upstream:** brand strategy Section 6 (10 non-negotiable strategic parameters); campaign goal (70 customers, 8 weeks, SGD 1,000 paid, trio at ~SGD 50–54); DNA hard constraints (3 flavour variations, SGD 18–30/bag, ≥6-week freshness promise, English-primary, Singapore-only).

---

## Executive summary

**The maths dictates the approach.** SGD 1,000 paid budget can realistically drive ~35–45 first-time buyers at achievable Singapore DTC CPAs. To hit 70, the campaign must generate **25–35 additional customers from organic, earned, community, and referral channels** — roughly 3×–5× what a paid-only strategy would deliver from non-paid sources. This isn't a branding preference; it's a mathematical requirement.

The campaign is therefore structured as a **two-phase, multi-channel acquisition engine** built around a single hero offer: the trio sampler (~SGD 50–54, one bag from each tier).

- **Phase 1 (Weeks 1–4): Launch + proof harvest.** Paid acquisition drives the first ~20–25 customers. Early buyers are deliberately funnelled into a review/UGC capture system. Editorial pitches go out. Community engagement begins. The landing page and SEO foundation are live from day one.
- **Phase 2 (Weeks 5–8): Proof-amplified scaling.** Social proof from Phase 1 buyers is deployed publicly — on the landing page, in paid creative, in organic content. Word-of-mouth and editorial inclusion (if secured) begin compounding. Paid spend shifts toward retargeting and search-intent capture as organic discovery channels mature.

**The trio sampler is the campaign's central organising device** — not just the offer but the acquisition mechanism. It de-risks the first purchase (three tiers, one decision), demonstrates the entire curation system, and gives every behavioural segment a reason to buy (adventurers get novelty, upgraders get a tasting flight, replenishers find their go-to bag). Every channel — paid ad, search result, editorial mention, community referral — points to the trio.

**The binding constraint is trust.** With no testimonials at launch, the campaign compensates with the freshness guarantee as risk-reversal, radical process transparency, and a mandated social-proof harvest by week 4. The guarantee must be structurally inseparable from the trio offer — wherever the trio appears, the ≥6-week freshness promise must be in the same decision frame.

## 1. Campaign approach

### 1.1 The core mechanism: a discovery funnel centred on the trio sampler

```
Awareness → Consideration → First Purchase (Trio) → Proof Harvest → Repeat/AOV
    ↑              ↑                ↑                    ↑              ↑
  Paid social   Search +        Landing page         Post-purchase   Tier-based
  + editorial   community       + guarantee           review/UGC      replenishment
  + organic     + referral      as risk-reversal      capture         path
```

**Why this structure:**

- **The trio is the trust engine, not just the hero offer.** For a cold audience meeting an unknown brand, the trio's architecture — three distinct tier experiences, one low-risk purchase, and an explicit freshness guarantee — *is* the risk-reversal. The campaign doesn't ask a sceptic to gamble on one bag from an unfamiliar brand; it invites them to experience the entire system in a single, reversible decision. **[DNA pain points (freshness gamble, taste gamble); brand strategy §2, §6.3]**
- **A single-offer funnel simplifies every channel.** Paid ads, search results, editorial mentions, community referrals — all converge on one landing page, one offer, one conversion event. This concentrates budget, reduces creative fragmentation, and makes performance measurement unambiguous. **[Goal: 70 customers; research §5: organic dependency]**
- **The post-purchase proof harvest closes the trust loop.** Early buyers are the campaign's most valuable asset — their reviews, photos, and UGC become the social proof that converts Phase 2 sceptics. Without this step, the campaign stalls at ~45 customers. With it, organic and referral channels have credibility signals to amplify. **[Brand strategy §3, §6.5; research §4]**

### 1.2 Two-phase structure

**Phase 1 — Launch + proof harvest (Weeks 1–4)**

| Activity | Purpose | Owner (downstream) |
|---|---|---|
| Paid social (Meta/IG) — discovery campaigns targeting adventurers & upgraders | Drive first ~20–25 trio purchases; generate initial traffic for pixel/retargeting | Performance Marketing Agent |
| Google Search — brand + "coffee delivery Singapore" + Asian-origin terms | Capture search intent from editorial/community mentions as they begin appearing | Performance Marketing Agent |
| Editorial outreach — pitch to 3–5 listicle outlets (Honeycombers, TheSmartLocal, SETHLUI, Time Out, Best in Singapore) | Seed earned-media coverage for Phase 2; target at least 1–2 inclusions | Marketing Director / operator (out of scope for downstream agents) |
| Community engagement — participate genuinely in SG home-barista spaces (Homeground, Nylon, Fluid Collective, home-café groups) | Build credibility and referral pathways; not paid, not transactional | Marketing Director / operator |
| Post-purchase review/UGC capture — every trio buyer receives a review request + photo prompt within 7 days of delivery | Harvest social proof for Phase 2 deployment | Performance Marketing Agent (mechanism); Creative Director (prompt design) |
| Organic social — IG/TikTok content (bean origins, testing process, vacuum-sealing BTS) | Build organic reach; feed community credibility; provide shareable content | Creative Director → asset creation |
| Landing page + SEO foundation live | Ensure conversion path exists; capture search volume for Asian-origin and specialty delivery queries | Performance Marketing Agent + Creative Director |

**Phase 2 — Proof-amplified scaling (Weeks 5–8)**

| Activity | Purpose | Owner (downstream) |
|---|---|---|
| Paid social — refresh creative with customer reviews/UGC from Phase 1 | Increase conversion rate with social proof; expand to replenisher-targeted messaging | Performance Marketing Agent |
| Paid search — increase budget share as organic mentions drive search volume | Capture intent from editorial coverage and word-of-mouth | Performance Marketing Agent |
| Retargeting — deploy against Phase 1 visitors who didn't convert | Recover warm-but-unconverted traffic | Performance Marketing Agent |
| Organic social — deploy UGC, reviews, and customer stories | Fuel community word-of-mouth; provide shareable social proof | Creative Director |
| Referral mechanism active — post-purchase "share your trio" prompt drives peer acquisition | Convert early buyers into acquisition channels | Performance Marketing Agent |
| Performance review (end of week 8) | Measure against all three KPI tiers; feed learnings into next campaign | Marketing Director |

**Why two phases:** Phase 1 generates the proof the campaign lacks at launch. Phase 2 deploys that proof to convert the more sceptical buyers (replenishers, cautious first-timers) and to fuel the organic/earned flywheel. Without the phase split, the campaign attempts to convert all 70 customers with no social proof — a structurally weaker position. **[Brand strategy §6.5; research §4, §5]**

### 1.3 How the three behavioural segments are reached

| Segment | Primary channels | Secondary channels | Conversion trigger | Phase emphasis |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Flavour-adventurers** (~25–30 of 70) | Paid social (discovery), editorial (novelty angle), community (enthusiast hubs) | Search (Asian-origin queries), organic social | Novelty of Asian origins + Tier 3 "exciting & unknown" framing | Phase 1 (highest-intent, converts earliest) |
| **Gear-led enthusiasts / upgraders** (~20–25 of 70) | Paid social (gear/brew content), community (Homeground, Nylon), search (specialty terms) | Editorial, organic social | Tasting-flight experience across all 3 tiers; curation as expertise | Both phases (consistent presence) |
| **Convenience-seeking replenishers** (~20–25 of 70) | Search (intent: "coffee delivery Singapore"), referral/word-of-mouth | Paid social (Phase 2, with social proof), editorial | Tier 1 "find your go-to bag" framing; guarantee as reliability promise | Phase 2 (needs social proof to convert; reached via search and referral, not discovery ads) |

**Why this segmentation matters for the target:** the prior campaign could hit 50 customers by focusing primarily on adventurers and upgraders — the highest-intent, most discovery-receptive segments reachable via paid. To hit 70, replenishers *must* convert, and they are structurally harder to reach via paid discovery ads. Search, referral, and editorial are their primary pathways — which is why organic/earned channels are mathematically necessary, not optional. **[Research §1, §5; brand strategy §6.4]**

### 1.4 Risk-reversal architecture

The campaign's trust deficit (no testimonials, unknown brand) is addressed through four layered mechanisms:

1. **The freshness guarantee (primary lever).** "Fresh for ≥6 weeks, or it's on us" — carried explicitly in every paid ad, on the landing page, in every editorial pitch. This is the hardest-to-copy claim and the strongest owned proof point. **[DNA #2; brand strategy §2, §5]**
2. **The trio structure (secondary lever).** The 3-tier framework makes the curation promise self-demonstrating — the buyer doesn't need to trust Coast's taste; they can see the system and choose their own comfort level. **[DNA #1; brand strategy §2]**
3. **Process transparency (tertiary lever).** Visual documentation of testing, degassing, and vacuum-sealing — shown in ads, on the landing page, in organic content. The buyer can verify quality themselves. **[Brand strategy §5]**
4. **Social proof harvest (compounding lever, weeks 4–8).** Real customer reviews and UGC deployed publicly — transforms the trust equation for Phase 2. **[Brand strategy §6.5]**

## 2. Channels at a glance

### 2.1 Paid channels

| Channel | Role in the campaign | Primary segment reached | Phase 1 allocation | Phase 2 allocation |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Meta/IG Ads** | Cold-audience discovery; Tier 2/3 messaging (adventurers, upgraders) | Adventurers, upgraders | ~SGD 350 | ~SGD 150 (shift: UGC creative, replenisher messaging) |
| **Google Search Ads** | Intent capture: "coffee delivery Singapore," "specialty coffee beans Singapore," "Asian coffee Singapore," brand terms | Replenishers, upgraders | ~SGD 100 | ~SGD 200 (increase as editorial/community drives search volume) |
| **Retargeting (Meta/Google)** | Recover landing-page visitors who didn't convert | All segments | ~SGD 50 (pixel warming) | ~SGD 150 (warm audience, social proof deployed) |

**Total paid: SGD 1,000** (~SGD 125/week average; front-loaded toward discovery in Phase 1, rebalanced toward intent and retargeting in Phase 2).

**Why Meta/IG leads (50% of spend):** Instagram is the dominant visual/discovery surface for SG café and coffee culture. **[Research §5]** The trio sampler and Asian-origin story are inherently visual and scroll-stopping — bean close-ups, origin landscapes, the vacuum-seal detail — making paid social the most efficient awareness driver within the budget. Meta/IG also offers the strongest interest-based targeting for adventurers (specialty coffee, pour-over, home brewing) and upgraders (coffee gear, grinders, brew methods).

**Why Google Search is weighted toward Phase 2 (30% overall, back-loaded):** search captures intent *generated by other channels* — editorial mentions, community referrals, word-of-mouth. It is lower-volume at launch (Coast has no brand search traffic) but compounds as the campaign progresses. The back-loaded allocation matches the expected intent curve. **[Research §5: SEO/search as compounding channel]**

**Why retargeting is small but present:** with a SGD 1,000 budget, retargeting pools will be small. But recovering even 3–5 customers from warm traffic significantly improves blended CAC. The Phase 2 increase coincides with social proof deployment — retargeting a warm audience that now sees reviews is higher-converting than retargeting the same audience at launch.

### 2.2 Organic, earned & community channels (SGD 0 budget, effort only)

| Channel | Role | Target outcome | Critical dependency |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Editorial pitches** (Honeycombers, TheSmartLocal, SETHLUI, Time Out, Best in Singapore) | Earned discovery; trust transfer from established publisher to unknown brand | At least 1–2 inclusions in "best roasters/coffee subscriptions" roundups during the 8-week window | The "Asian-origin discovery roaster with a 3-tier system and freshness guarantee" story is pitchable — it's a genuine trend story, not manufactured. **[Brand strategy §6.6; research §5]** |
| **SG coffee community engagement** (Homeground, Nylon, Fluid Collective, home-café movement) | Credibility and word-of-mouth in high-intent enthusiast circles | Genuine community presence; informal referrals; social proof among the most influential early adopters | Requires authentic participation, not transactional outreach. **[Research §5]** |
| **Organic social** (IG, TikTok) | Content flywheel; shareable proof of testing, origins, and customer experience | Consistent posting cadence; UGC reposts in Phase 2; engagement rate ≥ 4% **[Goal Creative KPI]** | Content quality and consistency; dependent on Creative Director output |
| **Post-purchase referral mechanism** | Convert trio buyers into acquisition channels | Referral share rate from early buyers; contributes to 25–35 organic customers | Mechanism must be simple and prompted at the right moment (after first tasting, not at purchase). **[Research §5]** |
| **SEO / landing page content** | Capture search volume for Asian-origin and specialty delivery queries; compound without ongoing spend | Organic search traffic contributing to landing-page conversions | Landing page must exist at launch and be optimised for relevant terms. **[Research §5]** |

**Why these channels are not optional:** the mathematics of 70 customers at SGD 1,000 paid spend require 25–35 customers from non-paid sources. **[Research §5]** Paid alone cannot reach 70 — the budget would need to be ~SGD 1,750–2,000 at realistic Singapore DTC CPAs of ~SGD 22–28 per first purchase. The organic/earned/community layer is structurally necessary, so it is treated as core to the strategy, not a supplementary "nice to have."

## 3. Budget allocation

### 3.1 Total budget: SGD 1,000 paid + SGD 0 organic/earned (effort only)

| Line item | Phase 1 (Weeks 1–4) | Phase 2 (Weeks 5–8) | Total | % of budget |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Meta/IG — discovery ads | SGD 350 | SGD 150 | SGD 500 | 50% |
| Google Search — intent + brand | SGD 100 | SGD 200 | SGD 300 | 30% |
| Retargeting (Meta + Google) | SGD 50 | SGD 150 | SGD 200 | 20% |
| **Total paid** | **SGD 500** | **SGD 500** | **SGD 1,000** | **100%** |
| Editorial outreach | SGD 0 (effort) | SGD 0 (effort) | SGD 0 | — |
| Community engagement | SGD 0 (effort) | SGD 0 (effort) | SGD 0 | — |
| Organic social (IG, TikTok) | SGD 0 (effort) | SGD 0 (effort) | SGD 0 | — |
| Referral mechanism | SGD 0 (effort) | SGD 0 (effort) | SGD 0 | — |

### 3.2 Phasing rationale

**Phase 1 (SGD 500, 50%):** front-loaded toward discovery — the campaign needs to generate traffic, build pixel data, and acquire the first cohort of buyers whose reviews will fuel Phase 2. Meta/IG leads (SGD 350) because cold-audience discovery is the primary paid job. Search is modest (SGD 100) because Coast has zero brand search volume at launch — this covers commercial-intent queries and begins building search presence. Retargeting is minimal (SGD 50) because pools don't exist yet; the spend warms the pixel.

**Phase 2 (SGD 500, 50%):** rebalanced toward intent capture and conversion. Search increases to SGD 200 as editorial mentions and community word-of-mouth begin driving search volume. Retargeting increases to SGD 150 as the visitor pool is now meaningful and social proof (reviews, UGC) is available to deploy in retargeting creative — the combination of warm audience + social proof is the campaign's highest-converting paid condition. Meta/IG discovery scales back to SGD 150 but shifts creative to UGC/testimonial-led formats and expands targeting to include replenisher-oriented messaging.

**Why equal split:** the budget is too small for dramatic front-loading. SGD 500 in Phase 1 is roughly what's needed to generate 20–25 trio purchases at estimated CPAs (see §4.2). Front-loading more would deplete Phase 2 before social proof exists to improve conversion rates. Equal split ensures both phases have enough fuel.

### 3.3 What the budget cannot do

- **Influencer paid partnerships:** at SGD 1,000 total, paid influencer posts are out of reach (SG nano-influencer rates start at ~SGD 100–300/post). Influencer reach, if pursued, must be via product seeding (gifted trios) — cost of goods, not paid budget.
- **Broad awareness campaigns:** the budget only supports tightly targeted conversion-oriented paid — broad reach/brand-awareness objectives would burn spend without attributable conversions.
- **Multi-creative testing at scale:** the budget supports 2–3 creative variations per channel, not extensive A/B testing. Creative must be high-quality from the start.

## 4. KPI tiers

### 4.1 Business KPI

| Metric | Target | Why this number | Measurement |
|---|---|---|---|
| **New customers acquired** | **70** in 8 weeks | Primary business objective from the goal. | Unique first-time purchasers of any Coast Coffee product. |
| **Blended CAC** | **≤ SGD 14.30** | SGD 1,000 ÷ 70 = SGD 14.29. If paid drives 40 customers, paid CAC = SGD 25; organic drives 30 at SGD 0, blended = SGD 14.29. This is the ceiling — lower is better, but the strategy is designed for this ratio. | Total paid spend ÷ total new customers (including organic). |
| **Paid CAC** | **≤ SGD 25** | At SGD 1,000 driving ~40 paid-attributed customers. Within the ≤ SGD 25 CPA from the goal. | Paid spend ÷ paid-attributed first purchases. |
| **AOV** | **≥ SGD 50** | The trio sampler at ~SGD 50–54 is the hero offer. AOV at or above SGD 50 confirms the trio is converting at target price. | Total revenue ÷ total orders (first purchases only; repeat excluded from this campaign's primary AOV). |
| **Trio attach rate** | **≥ 70% of first purchases** | The trio is the trust engine and the designed acquisition path. If <70% of first purchases are trios, the campaign's core mechanism isn't working — buyers are bypassing the discovery system for single bags, which weakens the curation differentiator and likely reduces repeat potential. | Trio orders ÷ total first-purchase orders. |

**Why blended CAC is the primary business metric (not paid CAC alone):** the campaign strategy deliberately invests effort (not budget) in organic/earned/community channels to acquire 25–35 customers. Holding the campaign to a paid-CAC-only standard would misrepresent the strategy — it would penalise the very organic acquisition the mathematics require. The blended CAC reflects the total resource investment (SGD 1,000 + effort) against the total outcome (70 customers).

## 6. Non-negotiable parameters (inherited from brand strategy §6)

These are binding constraints the campaign strategy is designed to honour. Downstream agents must not violate them.

1. **The whitespace is the union** — Asian-origin + 3-tier curation + ≥6-week guarantee must be structurally present together in every major touchpoint (landing page, primary ad creative, editorial pitch).
2. **The 3-tier framework is the central organising principle** — not a footnote or fine print. All three segments must find their entry point within it.
3. **The trio must exist and the guarantee must hold operationally.**
4. **All three segments must be addressed** — replenisher conversion is not optional for the 70-customer target.
5. **Social proof must exist by week 4** — the mid-campaign harvest is a campaign-execution dependency.
6. **The positioning must travel through editorial and community channels** — the story must be pitchable and shareable.
7. **Pricing is premium-justified, never price-led** — no discounting to hit the target.
8. **Asian origins are "ahead of the curve," never second-tier.**
9. **DNA constraints are absolute:** ≤3 flavour variations, SGD 18–30/bag, ≥6-week freshness not overstated, English-primary, Singapore-only.
10. **Voice is gated on customer validation** — Creative uses the provisional "knowledgeable guide" as a working assumption only.

## 7. What this strategy deliberately does NOT do

- **No discounting or promotional pricing.** Coast competes on curation, guarantee, and discovery — not cheapness. The trio's ~SGD 50–54 price is justified by discovery value, not a discount vs. per-bag pricing. **[Brand strategy §6.7; DNA price band]**
- **No subscription push in the first purchase.** The campaign's job is acquisition — get 70 first-time buyers to experience the trio. Subscriptions are a post-campaign retention play, once the customer has found their tier preference. Pushing subscriptions at first purchase dilutes the trio's "low-risk discovery" frame.
- **No broad/brand-awareness paid objectives.** At SGD 1,000, every paid dollar must be attributable. Conversion-oriented campaigns only.
- **No channel expansion beyond Meta/IG + Google Search + retargeting.** TikTok, LinkedIn, programmatic display, and influencer partnerships are out of scope for this budget and target.
- **No creative production.** This strategy defines the campaign architecture, channels, budget, and KPIs. Creative concepts, briefs, and asset prompts are the Creative Director's domain. Channel-level execution (audiences, bidding, ad formats, landing-page specifics) is the Performance Marketing Agent's domain.

## 8. Handoff to downstream stages

### 8.1 To Creative Direction

The campaign strategy establishes:
- **The hero offer:** the trio sampler (~SGD 50–54, one bag per tier) — the single thing all creative points toward.
- **The primary message (from brand strategy):** "Discover Asian coffee matched to your taste — guaranteed fresh, or it's on us."
- **The guarantee must be inseparable from the trio** — wherever the trio appears, the ≥6-week freshness promise must be in the same decision frame.
- **Three segments, three reasons to buy the trio** — creative should flex messaging across adventurer (novelty), upgrader (tasting flight), and replenisher (find your go-to bag) frames.
- **Two phases** — Phase 1 creative leads with the guarantee + Asian-origin novelty + process transparency; Phase 2 creative incorporates customer reviews and UGC from Phase 1.
- **Voice is provisional** — use the "knowledgeable guide" persona as a working assumption; await customer validation before locking final tone.
- **Creative KPIs:** hook rate ≥ 25% (paid social video), engagement rate ≥ 4% (organic).

### 8.2 To Performance Marketing

The campaign strategy establishes:
- **Budget:** SGD 1,000 across 8 weeks, split Meta/IG (50%), Google Search (30%), retargeting (20%).
- **Phasing:** SGD 500 per phase. Phase 1: Meta-led discovery (SGD 350), Search (SGD 100), Retargeting (SGD 50). Phase 2: Search-led intent (SGD 200), Meta (SGD 150), Retargeting (SGD 150).
- **Marketing KPIs:** landing page CVR ≥ 3%, paid CPA ≤ SGD 25, paid social CTR ≥ 1.0%, search CTR ≥ 3.0%.
- **Review/UGC capture mechanism** must be operational from day 1 — post-purchase emails/prompts to harvest proof for Phase 2.
- **Targeting:** Phase 1 focuses on adventurers and upgraders (specialty coffee, pour-over, home brewing interests); Phase 2 expands to replenisher signals (coffee delivery, subscription intent) and retargeting.
- **No broad or brand-awareness objectives** — conversion campaigns only.
