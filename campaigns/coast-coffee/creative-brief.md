## 1. Big idea / campaign theme

### The big idea: **"Three Ways In"**
*A guided journey from safe to bold, delivered fresh — pick how adventurous you feel.*

Coast doesn't sell you a coffee and hope you like it. It gives you **three doors into Asian coffee** — Tier 1 (safe), Tier 2 (complex/floral), Tier 3 (exciting/unknown) — and lets *you* choose how far in to step. The trio sampler is all three doors in one box, and the freshness guarantee means whichever door you open, what's inside arrives exactly as intended.

### Why this is the idea (rationale tied to the goal)
- **It dramatises the positioning's *combination*, not a single feature.** "Three Ways In" is literally the 3-tier curation (the spine of the strategy [STRATEGY]), it is inherently Asian-origin (the doors lead to China/Thailand/Indonesia/Malaysia [DNA #3]), and "delivered fresh… exactly as intended" carries the guarantee. The idea cannot be reduced to "fresh coffee" or "Asian coffee" alone — which is exactly the defensibility brand strategy demanded. [STRATEGY handoff #1]
- **It converts the nervous majority — the people the goal depends on.** A cold audience's two biggest frictions are unfamiliar origins and new-brand trust. [RESEARCH] "Three Ways In" hands control to the buyer ("you choose how adventurous"), so the safe on-ramp (Tier 1) is built into the headline idea, not buried as fine print. Lowering perceived risk on the first purchase directly serves CAC and the 70-customer target. [STRATEGY pillar 1]
- **It makes the trio self-explanatory and premium.** "Three doors in one box" frames the trio as *three experiences / a guided journey* — discovery value, never a discount. It justifies the ~SGD 50–54 price by its structure, not by cutting it. [STRATEGY]
- **It is legible in 3 seconds** — essential for the ≥25% hook-rate KPI. "Three Ways In" reads instantly as a visual system (three tiers, three beans, three doors), which makes the lead video's opening frame work without copy. [STRATEGY KPI]
- **It rides the trend tailwind without leaning on it as the sole reason.** The "doors" lead somewhere genuinely interesting (Asian origins most roasters skip), reframing unfamiliar → ahead-of-the-curve [STRATEGY handoff #6] — but discovery is *guided*, never a gamble.

### Campaign theme expression (the verbal/visual system, not finished copy)
- **Theme line (illustrative — Asset Prompt stage / copy finalises wording):** *"Three Ways In."* Supporting frame: *"Safe. Complex. Unknown. Pick your door."*
- **The tier ladder is the recurring visual motif** across every asset: three steps/doors/tiers shown in a consistent left-to-right (or bottom-to-top) progression — Tier 1 → Tier 2 → Tier 3 — so the system is recognisable at a glance and builds brand memory across touchpoints on a thin budget.
- **The guarantee is the recurring trust stamp** — a consistent "Fresh ≥6 weeks, guaranteed" lockup that appears on every commerce-intent surface, paired with the trio at the point of decision. [STRATEGY handoff #3]

## 2. Key message & supporting hooks

### Primary creative message (translating the approved messaging hierarchy)
**"Three ways into Asian coffee — pick how adventurous you feel, and it's guaranteed fresh when it lands."**
This is the creative expression of the approved primary message ("Discover Asian coffee matched to your taste — guaranteed fresh, or it's on us." [STRATEGY]). Wording is illustrative; the Asset Prompt stage / copy owns final phrasing.

### Supporting hooks (mapped to the approved supporting messages)
| Hook angle (illustrative) | Carries which approved message | Job |
|---|---|---|
| *"Safe, complex, or unknown — you choose your door."* | "Start safe or go bold — you choose." [STRATEGY] | Communicates the 3-tier system as an invitation; the on-ramp that converts the nervous buyer. |
| *"Coffee from China, Thailand and Indonesia — the origins most roasters skip."* | "Beans from China, Thailand, Indonesia and beyond…" [STRATEGY] | The novelty/differentiation hook; reframes unfamiliar → ahead-of-the-curve. |
| *"We test every bean, then guarantee it's fresh for at least 6 weeks."* | "We test every bean, then guarantee…" [STRATEGY] | The risk-reversal proof; carries the trust load with no testimonials. |
| *"All three tiers. One box. One easy way to find your coffee."* | "Try all three tiers in one box." [STRATEGY] | Makes the trio the low-risk entry; frames cost as discovery value. |

### Hooks engineered to win the ≥25% hook-rate KPI (first 3 seconds of the lead video)
*Hook model: open on a problem, a curiosity gap, or visible proof — never a logo or a slow brand build.* Provide **3 distinct opening hooks to A/B test**, so paid scales only behind the winning opener (per campaign strategy's "no spend behind unproven creative" rule [STRATEGY]):

1. **Proof-in-motion hook (lead recommendation [REC]).** Open in extreme close-up on the **vacuum seal being pulled / the degassing valve / beans hitting the bag**, with an on-screen line like *"This is why your beans arrive fresh — guaranteed 6 weeks."* — *Why:* leads with the hardest-to-copy, most differentiated proof; the physical action is inherently arresting and stops the scroll with a question already answered. Directly serves the trust gap. [STRATEGY]
2. **Curiosity / pattern-interrupt hook.** Open on three sealed bags labelled **"SAFE / COMPLEX / UNKNOWN"** with *"Which coffee person are you?"* — *Why:* the unexpected labels + a direct question create a curiosity gap and self-identification in <3s; dramatises the 3-tier idea instantly. [STRATEGY pillar 1]
3. **Reframe / contrarian hook.** Open on a familiar origin map crossed out, revealing **China / Thailand / Indonesia**, with *"Forget Ethiopia for a second."* — *Why:* a contrarian opener interrupts the category's default (global origins) and plants the Asian-origin differentiation immediately. Reframes unfamiliar → ahead-of-the-curve. [STRATEGY handoff #6]

> **Hook-rate guardrails:** the differentiating idea must be visible/legible by second 2–3 (brand mark can wait); motion or a question in frame one; no slow logo intro; captions on (sound-off viewing is the IG/Meta norm). [REC grounded in STRATEGY KPI]

## 4. Creative direction per asset type

### Global visual direction (applies to all)
- **Look & feel:** premium, warm, tactile, editorial — **not** discount/promotional. Think considered specialty-coffee craft: natural light, real beans, real hands, real equipment. Justifies SGD 18–30 visually. [STRATEGY: premium, never cheap]
- **The tier ladder motif** is the connective tissue: a consistent **Tier 1 → Tier 2 → Tier 3** progression (left-to-right or low-to-high), with a consistent visual language for "safe → complex → unknown" (e.g. calm/familiar → layered/textured → bold/mysterious). Recognisable at a glance across every asset to build memory cheaply.
- **Colour & typography [REC]:** a warm, grounded palette with one accent that signals each tier's mood; clean, confident type. (Brand visual identity is `<TO CONFIRM>` in DNA — flag for the Asset Prompt stage to confirm or propose.)
- **Captions always on** (sound-off-first for Meta/IG).

### Video (A1, A2, B1)
- **Direction:** hook in frame one (motion or question — see §2). Show the **physical proof** (seal, valve, beans, roast date) in close-up; show the **three tiers** as a clear visual system; land on the **trio box + guarantee + CTA**.
- **Tone:** the knowledgeable guide — plain-spoken, confident, specific; never hype.
- **Do:** lead with proof or curiosity in <3s; show real hands/process; caption everything; keep it 15–30s. **Don't:** open on a logo; use stock-feeling footage; bury the differentiator; overstate the guarantee.

### The 3-tier system, shown visually (all assets)
- **Always three, always in order, always labelled by mindset** (safe / complex / unknown), not by jargon. The viewer should grasp "pick how adventurous you feel" without reading body copy.
- Represent the tiers as **a journey/ladder/doors**, not a price grid — reinforces discovery value over discount. [STRATEGY]
- Each tier gets a **distinct but harmonised visual treatment** so the system feels curated, not random.

### The trio, shown (A3, B-series)
- **Hero shot = the opened box with all three bags visible in tier order**, so "three experiences in one box" reads instantly. Show it as a considered gift-like object (premium), with the tier labels legible.
- Pair the trio with the **guarantee lockup in the same frame** at any commerce-intent moment. [STRATEGY handoff #3]
- **Don't** show price-slash/"% off" treatments; frame value as *three distinct coffees / a guided journey*.

### Freshness & process, shown credibly (A4, A1)
- **Show, don't claim:** the testing, the degassing valve, the vacuum-seal being formed/pulled, the **roast/seal date** on the bag. Real, specific, verifiable — this is the transparency that substitutes for reviews. [STRATEGY]
- **Do:** macro/close-up of the seal and valve; show the date; state the guarantee in exact wording (see §5). **Don't:** imply "fresh forever," add unverifiable freshness claims, or stretch beyond ≥6 weeks. [DNA constraint]

### Static & carousel (A3, A4, A5, A6)
- One idea per asset; the differentiating combination legible without reading. Tier ladder or guarantee lockup present. Premium photography, not graphic-heavy promo design.

### Google Search (A7)
- Text-led; headlines must front-load the **differentiating combination** (Asian-origin + tiers + guaranteed-fresh) and the **trio**. Match high-intent query language from research ("buy coffee beans online Singapore," "Asian coffee beans," "coffee sampler Singapore"). [RESEARCH] No visual direction needed beyond sitelink structure.

### Landing page (A8)
- **Section order mirrors the approved hierarchy:** (1) hero — three ways in + trio; (2) the 3-tier ladder explained; (3) guarantee + visible process (risk-reversal); (4) Asian-origin story (ahead-of-the-curve); (5) trio offer + guarantee lockup CTA; (6) objection/FAQ block answering the four DNA objections; (7) **review module placeholder** (empty in Phase 1, populated in Phase 2).
- **Tone:** knowledgeable guide throughout. **Do:** put the guarantee adjacent to the buy button; make the tier choice obvious. **Don't:** load it with price-promotion language; don't leave a fake testimonial in the placeholder.

### Organic IG (A10) & editorial kit (A11)
- **Direction:** establish the brand world and the knowledgeable-guide POV — origin stories, the tier system, the process, the founder/sourcing perspective (if captured). Education + craft, designed for **saves/shares/comments** to hit ≥4% engagement. [STRATEGY KPI]
- **Do:** teach something in every post; invite "which tier are you?" interaction. **Don't:** hard-sell; don't post discount bait.

## 5. Mandatories & guardrails

### Brand voice cues (the knowledgeable guide — pending validation [REC])
- Knowledgeable, **not** gatekeeping; warm, plain-spoken; confident and unhedged **only** on the guarantee; honest and specific (country, process, tasting notes) over superlatives. Honour the full do's/don'ts table in brand strategy §4. [STRATEGY]
- **Never:** coffee jargon as a status flex; vague superlatives ("best coffee ever"); shaming the nervous buyer; framing Asian origins as cheap/exotic/gimmicky; apologising for or competing on price.

### Claims that must be exact
- **Freshness guarantee wording must not overstate the ≥6-week promise.** [DNA hard constraint] Use the structure approved in strategy, e.g. *"We test every bean, vacuum-seal it after degassing, and guarantee it's fresh for at least 6 weeks. If it's not, that's on us."* [STRATEGY example phrasing] — **"at least 6 weeks" / "≥6 weeks," never an unbounded or longer claim.**
- **Price:** present within the **SGD 18–30/bag** band; the trio as discovery value, never a discount/percentage-off. [DNA/STRATEGY]
- **Max 3 flavour variations** (Tier 1 / Tier 2 / Tier 3) — never imply more. [DNA]
- **English-primary (SG)** — Mandarin/Malay reach is unconfirmed; do not assume it. [DNA]

### No-fabrication mandate (critical)
- **There are zero customer testimonials, reviews, or results on file.** [DNA; RESEARCH] **Do not write, imply, or mock up any testimonial, star rating, "loved by X customers," or social-proof claim as if real.** All Phase 1 creative must work on **risk-reversal + visible process** alone. [STRATEGY]

### Testimonial-slot plan (for later phases)
- **Phase 1 (weeks 1–4):** design B1, B2, and the A8 landing-page review module with **clearly-marked empty slots** sized for review quotes / star ratings / UGC stills. Slots stay empty or are replaced by process-proof until real content exists.
- **Weeks 3–4:** harvest first-buyer reviews/UGC via a post-purchase request (mechanism owned by orchestrator/performance, flagged here per strategy). [STRATEGY §6]
- **Phase 2 (weeks 5–8):** populate the slots (B1, B2, B3) with **real** harvested content only, to scale proof toward the 70-customer target.

### Within-DNA recap
Max 3 tiers · SGD 18–30/bag · ≥6-week promise not overstated · English-primary · premium-never-discount · no fabricated proof. All honoured above.
