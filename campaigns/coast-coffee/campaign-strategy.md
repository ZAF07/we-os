# Campaign Strategy — Coast Coffee Launch

> Owned by the Marketing Director. Decides the campaign approach, channels-at-a-glance, and
> budget allocation. Grounded in `goal.md`, `customers/coast-coffee/dna.md`, `research.md`,
> and `brand-strategy.md`. Detailed channel setup, KPI instrumentation, and optimization
> are delegated to the performance-marketing stage; this document sets the frame they execute within.

## 1. Executive summary
A cold-start launch for an unknown premium brand on a thin budget (SGD 1,000 / 8 weeks). The
strategy is a **discovery-led acquisition play**: lead with the **3-tier flavour trio sampler**
as a low-risk first purchase, de-risk it with the **≥6-week freshness guarantee** (risk-reversal
that substitutes for the missing testimonials), and recruit demand from **high-intent search +
interest-targeted social + earned editorial/community**. The trio is the conversion event that
turns a curious browser into one of the 70 new customers.

## 2. Strategic approach & why
- **Hero offer = the trio sampler, not a discount.** The trio (one bean per tier) *is* the brand
  thesis made buyable: it demonstrates the 3-tier curation in a single SKU and lets a wary first
  buyer sample across the range instead of betting SGD 18–30 on one unfamiliar Asian origin.
  *Why:* directly attacks the two biggest first-purchase barriers (unfamiliar origins + new-brand
  trust) while protecting premium positioning (discovery value, never price-led).
- **Risk-reversal as the trust engine.** With no testimonials on file, the freshness guarantee +
  visible testing/degassing process carry the trust load. *Why:* research named trust as the
  weakest link; risk-reversal is the fastest substitute for social proof on a cold launch.
- **Own the whitespace in every touch:** Asian-origin + 3-tier curation + freshness guarantee —
  the one combination no competitor unites. *Why:* differentiation is the only affordable moat at
  this budget; undifferentiated spend would lose to established roasters.
- **Harvest proof during the run.** Treat the first cohort of buyers as a testimonial pipeline
  (post-purchase review/UGC request). *Why:* converts the trust weakness into an asset for weeks 4–8.

## 3. Channels at a glance (allocation frame — performance stage finalises setup)
| Channel | Role | Why (tied to goal) | Indicative budget |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Google Search (brand + non-brand specialty/Asian-coffee terms)** | Capture high commercial intent | Research: search is highest-intent path to a purchase; cheapest CAC for ready-to-buy | ~SGD 350 (35%) |
| **Meta/Instagram (interest-targeted: home brewing, specialty coffee, SG)** | Create demand + retarget | Audience confirmed on IG; carries the visual trio + freshness story; retargeting closes the wary | ~SGD 450 (45%) |
| **Organic IG + community (SG coffee groups/events) + editorial outreach** | Earned trust & reach | Honeycombers/TheSmartLocal/SETHLUI ecosystem + enthusiast communities build credibility a new brand can't buy | SGD 0 paid (effort) |
| **Retargeting reserve / scaling the winner** | Double down on what works | Hold budget to scale the lowest-CAC channel after week 3 read | ~SGD 200 (20%) |

Paid total: SGD 1,000. Expectation per `goal.md`: paid drives ~35–45 of the 70 new customers
(cost per purchase ≤ SGD 25); organic/earned + retargeting deliver the remainder → blended CAC ≤ SGD 14.

## 4. Budget logic
- **8-week cadence:** ~SGD 125/week average, but front-load lightly weeks 1–3 to gather conversion
  data across both paid channels, then reallocate the 20% reserve to the winner for weeks 4–8.
- **Floor discipline:** keep each test channel funded long enough for a valid read before cutting.
- **No spend behind unproven creative** — paid scales only after the hook-rate creative KPI is met.

## 5. KPI tiers (from goal.md — performance stage instruments these)
- **Business:** 70 new customers / 8 weeks; blended CAC ≤ SGD 14; AOV ≥ SGD 50 (trio).
- **Marketing:** landing-page CVR ≥ 3%; cost per purchase ≤ SGD 25; paid-social CTR ≥ 1.0%.
- **Creative:** hook rate ≥ 25% on lead freshness/origin video; organic engagement ≥ 4%.

## 6. Phasing
1. **Weeks 1–2 — Launch & learn:** trio offer live; both paid channels on; editorial/community outreach; instrument tracking.
2. **Weeks 3–4 — Read & reallocate:** cut losers, shift reserve to lowest-CAC channel; turn on retargeting; begin harvesting first-buyer reviews.
3. **Weeks 5–8 — Scale proof:** inject harvested testimonials/UGC into creative; scale the winning channel toward the 70-customer target.

## 7. Handoffs
- **Creative Director:** build the trio sampler as hero; lead with the freshness-guarantee risk-reversal and the 3-tier discovery story; honour the "knowledgeable guide" voice (to validate). See `brand-strategy.md` handoff.
- **Performance Marketing:** finalise channel setup, audiences, bid/budget pacing, tracking, and the week-3 reallocation rule against the KPIs above.
