## Executive summary

- **The market is real and growing.** Singapore coffee market ~USD 31.9M (2025), forecast ~8.2% CAGR to 2035, with home brewing explicitly named as a growth driver (remote work, lifestyle upgrades, rising quality appreciation). [FACT]
- **The category is crowded but the Asian-origin + curation + freshness-guarantee combination is genuine whitespace.** Established SG roasters (PPP, Common Man, Cowpresso, Perk, Hook, Tiong Hoe, Round Boy) compete largely on Latin American / African single origins, with freshness expressed as "freshly roasted / ships within X days of roast" — **none found makes an explicit ≥6-week post-degassing freshness *guarantee*, and none builds its brand on an Asian-origin curation system organised by adventurousness.** [FACT for competitor claims; INFERENCE for the whitespace conclusion]
- **Asian-origin specialists exist but are positioned differently.** Tanamera and JJ Royal focus on Indonesian origin as heritage/provenance, not as a curated "safe→adventurous" discovery journey across multiple Asian countries. This validates demand for Asian beans while leaving Coast's specific framing open. [FACT]
- **A 3-bean trio sampler is a category-validated acquisition mechanic** — competitors use surprise boxes, subscriptions, and trial-priced bags; Coast's trio is differentiated by mapping the three bags to its 3 flavour tiers. [FACT + INFERENCE]
- **Three behavioural sub-segments** map cleanly onto the DNA's 3-tier framework (see Segmentation). The **flavour-adventurer** is the sharpest fit for an Asian-origin discovery sampler. [INFERENCE grounded in DNA]
- **First-purchase friction for a cold audience centres on freshness-on-arrival and unfamiliar origins** — both of which Coast has concrete proof assets for (testing process + the guarantee). Trust-in-a-new-brand is the hardest objection given a CAC ≤ SGD 14 target and no testimonials yet on file. [INFERENCE; DNA flags testimonials as `<TO CONFIRM>`]

## 1. Target audience / segmentation
*Method: behavioural audience segmentation + JTBD.*

### Who SG home baristas are (context)
- Singapore has a mature, mainstream specialty coffee culture (specialty took off early 2010s) and an active home-brewing movement driven by remote/hybrid work, lifestyle upgrades, and rising appreciation for coffee quality. Consumers are investing in grinders, scales, pour-over kits, frothers and even home roasters. [FACT]
- Specialty adoption is **"accelerating beyond expat circles,"** with local consumers showing growing sophistication in single-origin profiles and brewing technique. [FACT] — i.e. the addressable audience is broadening from expats/early adopters to locals.
- Engaged enthusiasts congregate around competitions and community-led roasters: the **Singapore National Coffee Championship** (Singapore Coffee Association) drew 71 competitors across hand-brew filter, espresso, latte art, and coffee tasting in 2025; community-builder roasters include **Homeground Coffee Roasters** (Joo Chiat, explicitly aims to "build a community of home brewers"), **Nylon Coffee Roasters** (Everton Park), and **Fluid Collective**. A **home-café movement** (HDB-based home setups) is also documented. [FACT]

### Three behavioural sub-segments (mapped to DNA tiers)
These are **[INFERENCE]** segments, constructed from the behavioural patterns above and aligned to the DNA's 3-tier framework. Sizing of each is a **gap** (no SG-specific segment-size data found — see gaps).

1. **Gear-led enthusiasts ("the upgraders").** Own/are buying grinders, scales, pour-over kits. Value provenance, transparency (process, varietal, roast date), and brew control. Lower price sensitivity for distinctive lots; high knowledge. Hang out around community roasters, competitions, brew-method content. *Maps to Tier 2/3 (complex-floral → exciting-unknown).* JTBD: *"Help me brew something interesting and impressive at home and keep discovering."* [INFERENCE]
2. **Convenience-seeking subscribers ("the replenishers").** Brew daily, value not running out and not having to travel to buy; the DNA's explicit "prefer beans delivered" cohort. Moderate price sensitivity; loyal once a routine forms. Reachable via subscription/DTC channels and search. *Maps to Tier 1 (basic & safe) as an entry, with upsell potential.* JTBD: *"Keep good, reliable, fresh coffee arriving so my morning ritual never breaks."* [INFERENCE, grounded in DNA pain point "convenience of home delivery"]
3. **Flavour-adventurers ("the explorers").** Actively seek novel origins, processes (e.g. the documented rise of mosto/anaerobic-fermented lots), and "something I haven't tried." Lower price sensitivity *for novelty*, higher for repeat staples. Most responsive to "exciting & unknown" Asian origins. *Maps directly to Tier 3, and to the trio sampler as a whole.* JTBD: *"Surprise me with coffees I can't easily get and tell me what makes them special."* [INFERENCE]

### What triggers a first purchase (audience-level)
- A **low-risk way to try** before committing (sampler/trial pricing) — category norm (see competitors). [FACT]
- **Removing the freshness gamble** of mail-order beans. [DNA pain point; INFERENCE on weight]
- **Novelty/discovery** framed with enough guidance not to feel like a gamble of taste — the 3-tier "pick by adventurousness" device directly answers this. [INFERENCE grounded in DNA differentiator #1]

### Whitespace for Coast Coffee (findings, not strategy) [INFERENCE built on FACTs above]
1. **Multi-country Asian-origin curation.** Asian-origin players found (Tanamera, JJ Royal) are **single-country Indonesia specialists**; mainstream roasters lead with Latin American/African origins and carry Asian beans only occasionally (e.g. CMCR's Thailand lot). No competitor was found building its brand on a **China + Thailand + Indonesia + Malaysia** spread.
2. **An explicit freshness *guarantee*.** Competitors signal freshness via *roast recency* ("48 hours / 7 days from roast," "roasting daily"). Coast's **≥6-week post-degassing, vacuum-sealed guarantee** is a structurally different, mail-order-specific promise — no equivalent found.
3. **A named curation system tied to adventurousness.** CMCR offers a quiz and PPP an omakase, but **no competitor offers a fixed, legible "Tier 1 safe → Tier 3 unknown" framework** as the organising idea. This is Coast's most defensible differentiator.
4. **Pricing context:** Coast's SGD 18–30 band sits at the **upper-middle** of the market (Hook from $14; PPP/CMCR/Cowpresso ~$17–28.50). The trio at ~SGD 50–54 is competitive vs. buying three single bags but is **not** an undercut play — it must be justified by curation + guarantee, not price. [INFERENCE]

## 3. Market & trends
*Method: trend analysis.*

- **Market size & growth:** Singapore coffee market ~**USD 31.91M (2025)**, forecast ~**8.2% CAGR to ~USD 70.18M by 2035**; described as entering a "premium-driven expansion." [FACT]
- **Home brewing is a named growth engine:** driven by lifestyle upgrades, remote/hybrid work, and rising appreciation for coffee quality; consumers buying grinders, scales, pour-over kits, frothers, home roasters. [FACT]
- **Premiumisation + convenience converging:** ground/pods and DTC delivery are growing as time-pressed professionals seek café quality at home — supports a DTC bean model. [FACT]
- **Asian-origin coffee is a rising trend specifically:**
  - Asia-Pacific is projected the **fastest-growing coffee region 2025–2030**; single-origin/specialty/organic in APAC forecast ~7.45% CAGR through 2031. [FACT]
  - **Thailand:** a mosto-fermented coffee from Sirinya (Northern Thailand) was a top-selling filter coffee in 2025; beans from Thailand, Myanmar, China and India show rising quality/interest year on year. [FACT]
  - **China/Yunnan:** rapid specialty growth, government support for Yunnan cultivation; China **doubled specialty green-bean imports 2020–2025**. [FACT]
  - Industry commentary expects **interest in Asian coffees to keep growing into 2026**. [FACT] — directly supportive of Coast's sourcing thesis and an 8-week launch window in mid/late 2026.
- **Seasonality:** No SG-specific bean-demand seasonality data found. Industry-event anchors exist — **Singapore Coffee Week** and the **Singapore National Coffee Championship** — which concentrate enthusiast attention and could co-time a launch. [FACT for events; seasonality = gap]

## 5. Channel intelligence (factual findings only — allocation is the performance agent's call)
*Method: channel/community mapping. Findings, not a media plan.*

**Where this audience is reachable in Singapore:**
- **Search (Google):** Strong commercial-intent behaviour exists — ranked listicles ("best coffee roasters/beans Singapore," "coffee subscription Singapore," "buy coffee beans online Singapore") are abundant and competitive, indicating people actively search to buy/compare. SEO + paid search are live channels; specialty roasters compete here. [FACT]
- **Editorial / listicle ecosystem:** High-traffic SG publishers shape discovery — **The Honeycombers, Best in Singapore, TheSmartLocal, Time Out Singapore, SETHLUI, DanielFoodDiary, Quality Life Coffee**. Inclusion in "best roasters / best coffee subscriptions" roundups is a recurring acquisition path for competitors (Hook, PPP all appear). [FACT]
- **Community & events (high-intent enthusiasts):** **Singapore Coffee Association / National Coffee Championship**, **Singapore Coffee Week**, and community roasters that convene home brewers — **Homeground (Joo Chiat), Nylon (Everton Park), Fluid Collective (Potong Pasir)**. The **home-café movement** is an emerging cultural pocket. [FACT]
- **Social:** Instagram is the dominant visual/discovery surface for SG café & coffee culture (cafe/home-café content is heavily Instagram-led per multiple sources). [FACT] TikTok and Facebook are plausible but **not confirmed** in this research for this niche.
- **Reddit / Telegram:** A SG home-barista presence on Reddit (r/singapore) and Telegram is **plausible but unverified** — searches did not surface specific active groups. **Gap** (verify directly on-platform before relying on them).

> Note: the SGD 1,000 / 8-week budget (~SGD 125/week, CAC ≤ ~SGD 14) is a hard constraint that will shape which of these channels are viable — but channel allocation is explicitly the **performance-marketing** agent's decision, not this report's.

## Implications for positioning (handoff notes for the Brand Strategy Agent — NOT positioning itself)
*These are observations the next stage should weigh; I am deliberately not writing the positioning, value prop, or messaging.*

- The **single clearest ownable space** combines three things no competitor was found to unite: **(a) multi-country Asian-origin focus, (b) the 3-tier "safe→unknown" curation system, and (c) an explicit ≥6-week freshness guarantee.** Each is individually rare; together they appear unique in the SG set.
- The **3-tier framework doubles as an objection-handler** (lets nervous buyers start safe) and a **segmentation device** (maps to the three behavioural sub-segments). Brand strategy may want it as a central organising idea, not just a product taxonomy.
- **Trust is the weakest link** for a cold launch (unknown brand vs. established names; no testimonials on file). The guarantee/testing process are the available risk-reversal assets; strategy should lean on them and plan to harvest social proof during the run.
- Pricing is **premium-justified, not price-led** — positioning should not compete on cheapness (Hook owns the low end at $14).
- The **Asian-origin trend tailwind (Thailand, Yunnan, APAC growth) is a credibility/timeliness asset** strategy can use to reframe "unfamiliar origins" from risk to "ahead of the curve."
