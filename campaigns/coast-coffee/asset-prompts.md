## Mandatories carried into every prompt (from the brief §5 + DNA)
- **Exact freshness wording only:** "**≥6 weeks, guaranteed**" / "fresh for **at least 6 weeks**." Never "fresh forever," never longer or unbounded. Lockup text on commerce surfaces: **"Fresh ≥6 weeks, guaranteed."**
- **Max 3 flavour variations** — Tier 1 (safe), Tier 2 (complex/floral), Tier 3 (exciting/unknown). Always three, in order, labelled by mindset (safe / complex / unknown). The trio sampler is the hero.
- **Premium, never price-led.** No discount, "% off," price-slash, or sale framing. Price band SGD 18–30/bag; trio framed as discovery value (three experiences), not a price.
- **Asian-origin focus:** China / Thailand / Indonesia / Malaysia.
- **English-primary (Singapore).**
- **No fabricated testimonials, reviews, ratings, names, or faces presented as real customers.** Phase-2 (B-series) and the A8 review module use clearly-marked empty PLACEHOLDER slots, filled only with real harvested content.
- **Voice = the knowledgeable guide:** knowledgeable-not-gatekeeping, warm, plain-spoken, confident (and unhedged) only on the guarantee, honest/specific over superlatives.

## Visual-identity assumption (flagged)
The brief flags brand visual identity (colour, type, logo) as `<TO CONFIRM>` (brief §4 / Knowledge gap 2). All image/video prompts therefore specify a **principle-level look** consistent with the brief's "global visual direction" (premium, warm, tactile, editorial; natural light; real beans/hands/equipment; tier-ladder motif). **Assumption to confirm with customer:** a warm, grounded palette (warm neutrals, kraft/stone, deep espresso-brown) with one mood accent per tier (Tier 1 calm sand/sage · Tier 2 layered amber/rose · Tier 3 deep indigo/charcoal). Swap palette/type once identity is confirmed. Pixel specs are current standard platform specs and should be re-confirmed against live platform guidance (brief §3 / Knowledge gap 1).
