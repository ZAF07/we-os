## 1. Campaign Approach

### 1.1 Why a Two-Phase Test → Scale Model

The budget (SGD 1,000 / 8 weeks) is too small to fund a broad, always-on campaign across multiple channels simultaneously. It is large enough to run a disciplined testing program that identifies what works and then concentrates spend behind it.

| Constraint | Implication | Strategic Response |
|------------|-------------|-------------------|
| SGD 125/week average | Cannot sustain more than 1–2 active paid ad sets at meaningful daily spend | Run 2–3 creative variants in Phase 1 at SGD 15–20/day each; kill losers, scale winners in Phase 2 |
| 70 new customers in 8 weeks | Paid alone cannot deliver 70 at SGD 1,000 (would require SGD 14.29 CPA — below typical Singapore specialty DTC CPAs of SGD 20–35) | Paid targets 35–40 customers; organic + referral must deliver the remaining 30–35 |
| No existing audience / brand awareness | Coast Coffee has no established social presence, no email list, no lookalike seed audience | Phase 1 must build the pixel, test audiences, and generate initial social proof simultaneously |
| Single hero offer (trio sampler) | Simplifies creative, landing page, and conversion tracking | All paid campaigns point to the trio purchase; creative only needs to sell one proposition |

### 1.2 The Strategic Flywheel for This Campaign

```
PAID SOCIAL (Meta)
  → Drives discovery & first visit
  → Converts → Trio Sampler purchase (primary path)
  → Retargets non-buyers with freshness/objection content
        ↓
ORGANIC SOCIAL (IG + TikTok)
  → Serves as social proof for paid visitors who check the brand
  → Generates discovery via algorithm when content resonates
  → Builds the audience pixel can later retarget
        ↓
POST-PURCHASE (Referral + Retention)
  → Trio box includes a "share with a friend" incentive
  → Follow-up email sequence converts trio buyers to single-bag reorders
  → Happy customers become organic advocates
```

**Why this flywheel:** At SGD 1,000, paid social can't be the only engine. But it can be the *ignition* — the spark that brings in early customers whose experience then fuels organic growth. The trio sampler is designed for this: one purchase, three tiers experienced, clear path to reorder. Every trio sold is both a revenue event and a marketing investment — the customer now knows which tier they prefer and is more likely to tell someone about it.

### 1.3 Phase Breakdown

#### Phase 1: Test (Weeks 1–4)

| Element | Detail |
|---------|--------|
| **Objective** | Identify the highest-converting creative × audience combination. Build initial social proof (followers, posts, a few reviews). Generate first 20–25 customers. |
| **Budget** | SGD 400 (SGD 100/week) |
| **Paid focus** | 3 creative variants × 2 audience segments. Conversion objective (purchase). Daily budget SGD 15/day per winning variant after day-7 cull. |
| **Organic focus** | Establish the brand's visual identity and voice on Instagram and TikTok. Organic content serves two strategic purposes: (a) social proof for paid visitors who verify the brand before purchasing, and (b) algorithmic discovery. The Creative Director determines the content approach, themes, posting cadence, and specific formats — grounded in the brand strategy and the Creative Director brief (§7). |
| **Community focus** | Identify and engage in 2–3 Singapore coffee enthusiast groups (Facebook, Reddit). No hard sell — answer questions, share origin knowledge, build presence. |
| **Success gate** | By end of Week 4: ≥20 customers acquired, ≥1 creative variant with CPA ≤ SGD 25, ≥1 audience segment with CPA ≤ SGD 25. If no variant clears SGD 25 CPA, pause and re-evaluate creative before Phase 2. |

#### Phase 2: Scale (Weeks 5–8)

| Element | Detail |
|---------|--------|
| **Objective** | Scale winning creative/audience combinations. Activate referral program. Accelerate organic content. Reach 70 total new customers. |
| **Budget** | SGD 600 (SGD 150/week) |
| **Paid focus** | Concentrate 80% of budget on the winning Phase 1 creative × audience combo. Remaining 20% tests one new variant. Introduce retargeting campaign for landing-page visitors who didn't purchase. |
| **Organic focus** | Accelerate organic presence. Activate early customer advocacy — encourage and repost user-generated content. The Creative Director determines the content approach and formats based on Phase 1 performance data and the Creative Director brief (§7). |
| **Referral activation** | Activate a referral program that rewards both the referring customer and the referred friend. The program's strategic purpose is to serve as the organic acquisition multiplier that closes the gap between paid-driven and organic-driven customers — the incentive structure should make the cost per referred customer significantly lower than the paid CPA. The Performance Marketing Agent designs the program mechanics and incentive structure within the SGD 150 referral budget. |
| **Success gate** | By end of Week 8: 70 new customers, blended CAC ≤ SGD 14, AOV ≥ SGD 50. |

## 2. Channels at a Glance

### 2.1 Channel Selection Rationale

Every channel choice traces to the Customer DNA or brand strategy research:

| Channel | Role in This Campaign | Why This Channel | Grounding |
|---------|----------------------|------------------|-----------|
| **Meta Ads (Instagram + Facebook)** | Primary paid acquisition. Conversion campaigns → trio sampler purchase. | Singapore home baristas are highly reachable via Meta's interest/behaviour targeting (specialty coffee, pour-over, home brewing). Visual format suits coffee. Lowest CPA for Singapore DTC at small budgets. | DNA: Singapore-local audience. Brand strategy research §2.1: competitors active on Instagram. Goal: paid expected to drive 35–45 of 70 customers. |
| **Organic Instagram** | Brand presence + social proof + discovery. | The "storefront" for a DTC brand without a physical café. Where potential customers go after seeing a paid ad to verify the brand is real. | DNA: primary segment includes home baristas who want *delivered* beans — they're online. Brand strategy §3: visual, sensory brand suits IG's format. |
| **Organic TikTok** | Secondary discovery channel. Algorithm-driven reach at zero media cost. | Coffee content performs well on TikTok. Low production barrier. Potential for outsized organic reach relative to follower count. | DNA: Singapore audience. Brand strategy §4.2: tailwind — "home brewing as ritual/hobby." TikTok rewards authentic process content. |
| **Coffee community groups** | Trust-building + word-of-mouth seeding. Zero cost. | Singapore has active specialty coffee communities on Facebook (e.g., "Singapore Coffee Lovers") and Reddit (r/singapore, coffee threads). Genuine participation builds credibility faster than ads for a new, small brand. | DNA: objection "Can I trust a new, small brand?" Brand strategy §3.3: Guide archetype — answer questions, share knowledge, don't hard-sell. |
| **Referral program** | Organic customer acquisition multiplier. | At SGD 14 blended CAC target, referrals must contribute 10–15 customers. | **Email (post-purchase)** | Retention + reorder. Not acquisition — but essential to campaign economics. | The trio sampler's strategic purpose is to convert first-time buyers into single-bag reorder customers. Email is the bridge: post-purchase sequence that guides the customer from "I tried all three" to "I know my tier — reorder." | Brand strategy §4.3: trio is the entry point, reorder is the goal. ## 3. Budget Allocation

### 3.1 Total Budget: SGD 1,000

| Category | Allocation | Amount | Rationale |
|----------|-----------|--------|-----------|
| **Meta Ads — Conversion** | 70% | SGD 700 | Primary acquisition engine. Conversion-optimised campaigns targeting trio sampler purchase. At SGD 20–25 CPA, delivers 28–35 customers. |
| **Meta Ads — Retargeting** | 10% | SGD 100 | Retarget landing-page visitors and video viewers who didn't convert. Lower-funnel, higher conversion rate. At SGD 10–15 CPA, delivers 6–10 additional customers. |
| **Referral incentives** | 15% | SGD 150 | Incentive budget for the referral program. The Performance Marketing Agent should design the incentive structure such that the cost per referred customer is substantially below the paid CPA, maximising customer yield from this allocation. Expected to drive 10–15 incremental customers. |
| **Community seeding** | 5% | SGD 50 | Cost of 2–3 trio sampler packs (COGS + delivery) sent to active Singapore coffee community members or nano-influencers. Zero paid promotion — the product is the "spend." Generates organic content and credibility. |

### 3.2 Paid Spend Cadence

| Phase | Weeks | Weekly Spend | Cumulative |
|-------|-------|-------------|------------|
| Phase 1 | 1–4 | SGD 100/week | SGD 400 |
| Phase 2 | 5–8 | SGD 150/week | SGD 600 |
| **Total** | **1–8** | | **SGD 1,000** |

Phase 2 receives 60% of budget because:
- Phase 1's purpose is to find what works, not to spend at scale.
- By Phase 2, the pixel has data, the winning creative is identified, and spend can be concentrated where it converts.
- Retargeting audiences only become meaningful after Phase 1 has driven traffic.

### 3.3 Customer Acquisition Model

| Source | Projected Customers | Implied Cost | Notes |
|--------|-------------------|--------------|-------|
| Meta — Conversion campaigns | 28–35 | SGD 20–25 CPA | SGD 700 budget. Conservative estimate at CPA upper bound. |
| Meta — Retargeting | 6–10 | SGD 10–17 CPA | SGD 100 budget. Retargeting typically converts at lower CPA. |
| Organic Instagram + TikTok | 10–15 | SGD 0 media cost | Discovery via algorithm + brand search from paid exposure. Hardest to predict; conservative estimate. |
| Referral program | 10–15 | Variable (per incentive design) | SGD 150 incentive budget. Requires ~20 early customers to refer. The Performance Marketing Agent determines the incentive structure; cost per referred customer should target well below the SGD 20–25 paid CPA. |
| Coffee communities + word-of-mouth | 5–10 | SGD 0 media cost | Slow build. Credibility-driven. |
| **Total** | **59–85** | | **Midpoint: 72 customers. Blended CAC: SGD 13.89** |

The model is deliberately conservative on organic and reliant on paid for the base. If organic overperforms, the blended CAC improves. If organic underperforms, paid can be topped up within the SGD 1,000 envelope (the retargeting and referral budgets have flexibility).

## 4. KPI Tiers

Every KPI ties to the business objective: **acquire 70 new customers in 8 weeks.**

### 4.1 Business KPIs

| KPI | Target | Measurement | Why This Target |
|-----|--------|-------------|-----------------|
| **New customers** | 70 | Unique first-time purchasers (Shopify/checkout) | The primary campaign objective. Non-negotiable. |
| **Blended CAC** | ≤ SGD 14 | Total campaign spend ÷ total new customers | SGD 1,000 ÷ 70 = SGD 14.29. Target rounds down to leave margin. If paid delivers 40 customers at SGD 25 CPA (SGD 1,000) and organic delivers 30 at SGD 0, blended CAC = SGD 14.29. |
| **AOV** | ≥ SGD 50 | Total revenue ÷ total orders | The trio sampler at SGD 50–54 is the hero offer. If AOV falls below SGD 50, too many single-bag purchases are occurring — the trio isn't converting. |
| **Gross revenue (minimum)** | SGD 3,500 | 70 customers × SGD 50 AOV | Revenue floor. Does not include repeat purchases within the 8-week window. |

**Business KPI hierarchy:** New customers is the primary KPI (it's the objective). AOV and blended CAC are guardrail KPIs — they ensure the *quality* and *efficiency* of acquisition. Hitting 70 customers at SGD 18 CAC with SGD 30 AOV is a failed campaign (wrong customers, wrong offer).

### 4.2 Marketing KPIs

| KPI | Target | Channel | Why This Target |
|-----|--------|---------|-----------------|
| **Cost per purchase (paid)** | ≤ SGD 25 | Meta Ads | At SGD 25 CPA with SGD 800 total Meta spend = 32 customers. This is the minimum viable paid contribution. Singapore specialty DTC benchmarks for new brands with no pixel data range SGD 20–35; SGD 25 is aggressive but achievable with a focused creative strategy. |
| **Landing page conversion rate** | ≥ 3% | Website/LP | Industry benchmark for DTC food & beverage landing pages is 2–5%. 3% is the floor for a new brand with no prior traffic. If CVR drops below 3%, the landing page — not the ads — is the bottleneck. |
| **CTR (paid social)** | ≥ 1.0% | Meta Ads | Meta average CTR for food & beverage is 0.8–1.2%. Below 1.0% signals creative or audience mismatch. |
| **Email capture rate** | ≥ 8% | Landing page | Capture non-buyers for retargeting and future campaigns. Pop-up or inline form. 8% is achievable with a simple "get brewing tips" or "learn about Asian origins" lead magnet. |
| **Referral activation rate** | ≥ 15% | Post-purchase | Percentage of customers who share their referral code at least once. 15% is conservative for a product people are excited about. |

**Marketing KPI hierarchy:** Cost per purchase is the lead Marketing KPI — it directly governs whether the paid budget can deliver its share of the 70-customer target. Landing page CVR is the second most important — it's the multiplier on every dollar of ad spend. CTR is a leading indicator; if it drops, CPA will follow.

### 4.3 Creative KPIs

Creative KPIs are leading indicators of conversion performance — they signal whether creative will convert before purchase data accumulates. The Creative Director defines the specific creative metrics and benchmarks based on the creative approach they develop, grounded in platform and category data. The Performance Marketing Agent tracks these against marketing and business outcomes.

**The Marketing Director's constraint:** Creative must support the downstream Marketing KPIs defined in §4.2 — specifically paid CTR ≥ 1.0% and CPA ≤ SGD 25. If creative underperforms against these downstream metrics, the Creative Director re-evaluates the creative approach using performance data from Phase 1.

The Creative Director brief (§7) provides the strategic parameters — target segment, hero offer, conversion goal, channel formats, CPA constraint — within which the Creative Director operates. Creative KPI selection and benchmarking are the Creative Director's remit (in collaboration with Performance Marketing on platform-specific benchmarks).

## 5. Strategic Risks & Mitigations

These are campaign-execution risks — distinct from the brand-level risks in the brand strategy (§5).

| Risk | Likelihood | Impact | Mitigation |
|------|-----------|--------|------------|
| **Meta pixel has no data at launch** | Certain | High — no lookalike audiences, no conversion-optimised delivery for first 50 conversions | Phase 1 starts with interest-based targeting (Singapore + specialty coffee/home brewing interests). Optimise for "Purchase" from day one even if delivery is slow — the pixel needs the signal. First 2 weeks will be learning phase; don't kill campaigns prematurely. |
| **Trio sampler economics don't work at SGD 50–54** | Low-Medium | High — the entire campaign is built on the trio as hero offer | Confirm trio COGS + delivery margin before launch. If trio is margin-negative, the campaign strategy must shift to single-bag acquisition. This is a pre-launch gate, not a mid-flight adjustment. |
| **Creative variants all underperform (CPA > SGD 25)** | Medium | High — paid engine stalls | Phase 1 success gate (end of Week 4): if no variant clears SGD 25 CPA, pause paid, re-brief Creative Director with performance data, and produce 3 new variants. Phase 2 delayed by 1 week if needed. SGD 400 of SGD 1,000 spent on learning is acceptable — the remaining SGD 600 must be deployed against proven creative. |
| **Organic content gains no traction** | Medium | Medium — paid must carry more weight, CAC rises | If organic reach is minimal by Week 3, shift strategy: use paid to boost top-performing organic posts (funded from the SGD 100 retargeting budget). This is a pragmatic hybrid — paid distribution for organic content. |
| **Referral program underperforms** | Medium | Medium — 10–15 customers short of target | If referral activation is below expectations by Week 6, the Performance Marketing Agent should deploy the contingency incentive structure designed as part of the referral program setup (e.g., an asymmetric incentive that weights the reward toward the referred friend to increase conversion). |
| **Competitor entry or price promotion during campaign** | Low | Medium | Coast Coffee's positioning (Asian-origin, 3-tier, freshness guarantee) is structurally distinct — competitor price promotions shouldn't directly undercut it. Monitor but don't react by discounting. |
