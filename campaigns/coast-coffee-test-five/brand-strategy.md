## 1. Positioning

### 1.1 Positioning Statement

> **For** Singapore home baristas who want beans matched to their taste without the guesswork,
> **Coast Coffee is** the specialty coffee brand that brings you the best beans Asia has to offer — organised in a simple 3-tier flavour system —
> **so you always know what you're getting.**
> **Unlike** typical roasters who treat Asian origins as an occasional footnote,
> **Coast Coffee is built on them**, with every bag degassed, vacuum-sealed, and guaranteed fresh for at least 6 weeks after delivery.

### 1.2 Why This Positioning

This positioning is built on three structural whitespaces identified in the competitive landscape — none of which any single competitor occupies:

| Positioning Pillar | Research Grounding | Competitive Evidence |
|---------------------|-------------------|----------------------|
| **Asian-origin focus as brand identity** | Research §2.2: "No competitor leads with Asian-origin focus. Every competitor… carries African and Latin American origins as their primary range. Asian origins… appear as occasional single-origin offerings — never as the brand's identity." | Hook Coffee, Nylon, CMCR, PPP, Homeground, Tiong Hoe, Round Boy, Yahava — all centre African/Latin American origins. |
| **3-tier flavour adventurousness system** | Research §2.2: "No competitor uses a simple 3-tier flavour-adventurousness system. Hook Coffee has a quiz, CMCR has 'Regional' vs 'UnCommon,' and Round Boy has 'Omakase' — but none frame their range as *safe → complex → exciting* tiers that guide the customer's own adventurousness journey." | Coast Coffee's second structural moat — curation without complexity, guidance without a quiz. |
| **≥6-week freshness guarantee (post-delivery shelf life)** | Research §2.2: "Freshness guarantees are surprisingly rare as explicit marketing claims. Only Hook Coffee ('within 7 days of roasting') and Tiong Hoe ('roast twice a week, ship within 5 days') publish delivery cadences. Coast Coffee's ≥6-week guaranteed shelf life… is about how long the beans *stay* fresh, not just how quickly they ship." | A different and more compelling angle than roast-to-ship speed. Addresses the #1 inferred objection: "Will the beans actually be fresh when they arrive?" |

These three pillars are mutually reinforcing: Asian origins are less familiar → the 3-tier system makes them approachable → the freshness guarantee removes the risk of trying something new by mail.

### 1.3 Positioning vs. Key Objections

The positioning is designed to pre-empt every inferred objection from the DNA:

| Objection (DNA) | How Positioning Answers It |
|------------------|---------------------------|
| "Will the beans actually be fresh when they arrive?" | The ≥6-week guarantee + degassing/vacuum-sealing story is the lead freshness claim. Not "we ship fast" but "they *stay* fresh." |
| "I don't know these Asian origins — are they as good?" | The 3-tier system reframes unfamiliarity as discovery. Tier 1 ("safe") is the entry point; Tier 3 ("exciting & unknown") turns the objection into an invitation. |
| "Why pay SGD 18–30 when the supermarket is cheaper?" | Asian-origin curation + freshness guarantee + home delivery = value supermarket commodity coffee cannot offer. The trio sampler at ~SGD 17–18/bag makes the premium tangible at lower risk. |
| "Can I trust a new, small brand?" | Specificity builds trust: named tiers, named origins, a concrete freshness number (≥6 weeks), a degassing + vacuum-sealing process. No vague "premium quality" claims. |

## 2. Messaging

### 2.1 Core Message

> **"Asia's best beans, your perfect match."**

This line works on three levels:
1. **"Asia's best beans"** — claims the Asian-origin whitespace definitively (Research §2.2, Pillar 1).
2. **"Your perfect match"** — speaks to the 3-tier preference-matching system (Research §2.2, Pillar 2) and directly addresses the Confident Beginner's core JTBD: "Get beans that match my taste preference without guesswork" (Research §1.1).
3. The line is warm, not clinical — fitting the Guide archetype (see §3).

### 2.2 Messaging Pillars

Each pillar maps to a specific research finding and addresses a specific customer need. The pillars are ordered by priority for the primary segment (Segment B: Confident Beginner — Research §5.2).

#### Pillar 1: Pick Your Flavour, Not a Mystery

> **"Three tiers. Safe, complex, or exciting. You choose how adventurous you want to be — no quizzes, no guesswork."**

| Attribute | Detail |
|-----------|--------|
| **Primary segment** | Segment B: Confident Beginner (overwhelmed by choice — Research §1.3) |
| **JTBD served** | "Get beans that match my taste preference without guesswork" (Research §1.1, Functional Job #1) |
| **Competitive moat** | The 3-tier gradient is structurally unique — no competitor frames curation as an adventurousness journey (Research §2.2, Pillar 2) |

#### Pillar 2: Freshness You Can Count On

> **"Every bag is degassed, vacuum-sealed, and guaranteed fresh for at least 6 weeks. Not 'roasted fast, shipped fast.' Actually fresh when you brew it."**

| Attribute | Detail |
|-----------|--------|
| **Primary segment** | Segment C: Convenience-First Buyer (freshness anxiety — Research §5.1) |
| **JTBD served** | "Want guaranteed-fresh beans — properly degassed, vacuum-sealed, long shelf life" (DNA; Research §1.1, Functional Job #2) |
| **Competitive moat** | The ≥6-week post-delivery guarantee is a different and more compelling claim than roast-to-ship speed (Research §2.2, Pillar 3) |
| **Objection addressed** | "Will the beans actually be fresh when they arrive?" — the #1 inferred objection (DNA + Research §1.3) |

#### Pillar 3: Asia in Your Cup

> **"Beans from China, Thailand, Indonesia, Malaysia — origins your coffee friends probably haven't tried. We source them, we test them, we stand behind them."**

| Attribute | Detail |
|-----------|--------|
| **Primary segment** | Segment A: Curious Explorer (novelty-seeking — Research §5.1) |
| **JTBD served** | "Want to discover interesting, less-common Asian-origin beans" (DNA; Research §1.1, Functional Job #3) |
| **Competitive moat** | Asian-origin focus as brand identity — no competitor leads with this (Research §2.2, Pillar 1) |
| **Objection addressed** | "I don't know these Asian origins — are they as good as the origins I know?" (DNA + Research §4.2: "Asian beans = lower quality" perception as a moderate-high headwind) |

#### Pillar 4: Delivered to Your Door

> **"Café-quality beans without the trip. Order online, brew at home."**

| Attribute | Detail |
|-----------|--------|
| **Primary segment** | Segment C: Convenience-First Buyer (time-poor — Research §5.1) |
| **JTBD served** | "Want the convenience of home delivery" and "Avoid the trip to a roaster/café" (DNA; Research §1.1) |
| **Competitive context** | Table stakes — nearly every competitor offers delivery (Research §2.1). This pillar supports rather than differentiates; it's the hygiene factor that must be present and frictionless, not the lead message. |

### 2.3 Messaging Hierarchy (For This Campaign)

```
CORE MESSAGE
"Asia's best beans, your perfect match."
─────────────────────────────────────────
PILLAR 1 (Lead)           PILLAR 2 (Lead)
Pick Your Flavour,        Freshness You
Not a Mystery             Can Count On
─────────────────────────────────────────
PILLAR 3 (Supporting)     PILLAR 4 (Hygiene)
Asia in Your Cup          Delivered to Your Door
```

**Rationale for this hierarchy:** For a campaign targeting the Confident Beginner (Segment B) as primary, the 3-tier system (Pillar 1) and freshness guarantee (Pillar 2) directly solve their two biggest anxieties — "what do I pick?" and "will it be fresh?" These two pillars carry the conversion weight. Pillar 3 (Asian origins) is the differentiator that makes them choose Coast Coffee *over* a familiar roaster. Pillar 4 (delivery) is expected — mention it, but don't lead with it.

### 2.4 Messaging by Segment

| Segment | Lead Pillar(s) | Hook Angle |
|---------|---------------|------------|
| **B: Confident Beginner** (Primary) | Pillar 1 + Pillar 2 | "Not sure which beans you'll like? We made it simple — 3 tiers, your choice. And they stay fresh for 6 weeks." |
| **C: Convenience-First** (Secondary) | Pillar 2 + Pillar 4 | "Fresh specialty coffee, delivered. Vacuum-sealed, guaranteed 6 weeks. No trip to the roaster needed." |
| **A: Curious Explorer** (Tertiary) | Pillar 3 + Pillar 1 | "Beans from Yunnan, Doi Chang, Sumatra — origins your coffee group hasn't debated yet. Start safe or go all-in." |

## 3. Brand Personality & Voice

### 3.1 Archetype: The Explorer × The Guide (Hybrid)

Coast Coffee sits at the intersection of two archetypes:

| Archetype | What It Brings | Research Grounding |
|-----------|---------------|-------------------|
| **The Explorer** | Discovery, novelty, adventure. Drives the Asian-origin story, Tier 3 ("exciting & unknown"), and the "your coffee friends haven't tried this" appeal. | Research §4.1 tailwind: "Rise of Asian-origin specialty coffee" + "Home brewing as ritual/hobby, not just cost-saving." Segment A: Curious Explorer is the purest expression of this archetype. |
| **The Guide** | Reassurance, navigation, trust. Drives the 3-tier system as a tool (not just a gimmick), the freshness guarantee as a promise, and the "we tested this, you'll like it" tone. | Research §5.2: Segment B (Confident Beginner) is the primary target — they need guidance, not just inspiration. The Guide makes the Explorer's discoveries accessible. |

**Why a hybrid?** Pure Explorer brands (adventure, discovery, novelty) work for Segment A (Curious Explorer) but risk alienating Segment B, who wants reassurance. Pure Guide brands (safe, educational, trustworthy) work for Segment B but lack the excitement that makes Coast Coffee distinctive. The hybrid — "we travel so you don't have to guess" — serves both.

### 3.2 Brand Personality Traits

| Trait | What It Means | What It Rejects |
|-------|---------------|-----------------|
| **Curious, not pretentious** | Genuine excitement about Asian coffee origins. "We found this amazing Yunnan — here's what it tastes like." | Coffee snobbery, gatekeeping, exclusivity. No "you probably haven't heard of it" energy. |
| **Warm, not clinical** | The tone of a friend who's really into coffee and wants you to enjoy it too. Approachable, enthusiastic. | Corporate, sterile, "premium quality" filler language. |
| **Confident, not arrogant** | Sure-footed about the product — the sourcing, the testing, the freshness. "Here's why this works." | Defensiveness about Asian origins. No apologising for being new or small. No over-promising. |
| **Precise, not pedantic** | Specific about origins, tasting notes, and process — because specifics build trust. | Jargon without explanation. Overwhelming detail that confuses rather than clarifies. |

### 3.3 Voice Guidelines

**Do:**
- Use sensory language: tasting notes, aroma descriptors, mouthfeel. Make the reader taste the coffee.
- Be specific about origins: name the farm, region, altitude, and process where possible. "Doi Chang, Chiang Rai" beats "Thailand."
- Speak in the first-person plural curation narrative: "We sourced," "We tested," "We loved this one because…" — this reinforces the Guide archetype (they did the work so the customer doesn't have to).
- Use simple, direct sentence structures. Short sentences. Clarity over cleverness.
- Acknowledge that Asian origins may be unfamiliar — and frame that as exciting, not risky.

**Don't:**
- **Coffee snobbery or gatekeeping.** Everyone is welcome at every tier. Avoid: "If you don't already know what anaerobic fermentation is…" or "Serious coffee drinkers already understand…" or "This one's not for beginners." Instead use: "Here's what anaerobic fermentation means — and why it makes this bean taste incredible."
- **Jargon without explanation.** Any technical term must earn its place by being explained in the same breath. Avoid: "This washed Ethiopian-style process delivers exceptional clarity." Instead use: "This bean went through a washed process — meaning the fruit was removed before drying — which gives it a cleaner, brighter taste."
- **Generic "premium quality" or "artisanal" claims without specifics.** Every quality claim needs a reason attached. Avoid: "Premium beans, artisanal roasting, exceptional quality." or "Hand-crafted with care." Instead use: "We cup every lot before we buy. If it doesn't score, it doesn't ship."
- **Over-promising.** The freshness guarantee is ≥6 weeks — never inflate it. The beans are excellent — never claim they're "the world's best" or "life-changing." Avoid: "The freshest beans in Singapore." or "You'll never go back to supermarket coffee." Instead use: "Guaranteed fresh for at least 6 weeks after delivery." and "If you brew at home, these are worth a try."
- **Competitor bashing.** The positioning differentiates without needing to name or diminish others. Avoid: "Unlike [Competitor X], our beans actually stay fresh." or "Most roasters ignore Asian origins — we don't." Instead use: "We built our whole range around Asian origins — it's what we're here for."

### 3.4 Voice Calibration by Segment

| Segment | Voice Adjustment |
|---------|-----------------|
| **B: Confident Beginner** | Lean Guide. More reassurance, more explanation. "Here's how the tiers work. Start with Tier 1 — you'll love it." |
| **A: Curious Explorer** | Lean Explorer. More origin storytelling, more sensory detail. "This Yunnan natural process is wild — strawberry, white tea, a hint of booziness." |
| **C: Convenience-First** | Lean Guide but shorter. Respect their time. "Fresh beans, your choice, at your door. Here's how it works in 3 steps." |

## 4. Value Proposition

### 4.1 Full Value Proposition

> **For** the Singapore home barista who wants beans matched to their taste without guesswork, guaranteed freshness they can rely on by mail, and discovery of interesting Asian origins their coffee friends haven't tried,
>
> **Coast Coffee delivers** a curated 3-tier trio of specialty Asian beans — degassed, vacuum-sealed, and guaranteed fresh for at least 6 weeks after delivery —
>
> **so you can explore** from safe to exciting, at your own pace, from your own kitchen.
>
> **At SGD 18–30 per bag** (or ~SGD 50–54 for the introductory flavour trio), you're paying less per cup than any café — and tasting something genuinely new.

### 4.2 Why This Value Proposition

Every element traces to a research finding:

| VP Element | Research Grounding |
|-------------|-------------------|
| "Beans matched to their taste without guesswork" | JTBD #1 (Research §1.1); Segment B pain point: overwhelming choice (Research §1.3); 3-tier system whitespace (Research §2.2) |
| "Guaranteed freshness they can rely on by mail" | JTBD #2 (Research §1.1); freshness guarantee as unique market claim (Research §2.2); addresses #1 objection |
| "Discovery of interesting Asian origins" | JTBD #3 (Research §1.1); Asian-origin whitespace (Research §2.2); Segment A appeal |
| "Degassed, vacuum-sealed, guaranteed ≥6 weeks" | DNA hard constraint; freshness process as trust-builder; distinct from competitor roast-to-ship claims |
| "Explore from safe to exciting, at your own pace" | 3-tier system as a journey, not just a product feature; serves both Segment B (start safe) and Segment A (go exciting) |
| "SGD 18–30 per bag / ~SGD 50–54 trio" | DNA price band; trio pricing competitive vs. individual bags (Research §2.2: trio at ~$17–18/bag is price-competitive) |
| "Less per cup than any café" | Research §3.1: Singapore café specialty coffee is SGD 6–8/cup; a bag yields ~12–15 cups → ~SGD 1.50–2.50/cup |

### 4.3 Value Proposition for the Trio Sampler (Campaign Lead Offer)

The trio sampler is the physical expression of Coast Coffee's entire value proposition in one purchase. Its specific value prop:

> **"One box, three tiers, your perfect match.**
> Taste the full Coast Coffee range — Tier 1 (safe), Tier 2 (complex & floral), Tier 3 (exciting & unknown) — in a single trio pack. Find your favourite. Reorder what you loved. ~SGD 50–54 for three 200 g bags, delivered."

**Why the trio drives the campaign objective (70 new customers):**

- It reduces first-purchase risk: the customer doesn't have to commit to one tier (Research §6, Synthesis #4).
- It lets the 3-tier system prove itself in the customer's own cup — the best possible conversion to repeat purchase.
- At ~SGD 17–18/bag, it's price-competitive with single-bag purchases from Nylon, PPP, or Yahava (Research §2.1), removing price as a barrier to trial.
- It directly serves the Confident Beginner's need for guidance: "Start here. Find your tier."

## 5. Strategic Risks & Mitigations

| Risk | Likelihood | Mitigation in This Strategy |
|------|-----------|---------------------------|
| **"Asian beans = lower quality" perception persists** | Moderate-High (Research §4.2) | Pillar 3 messaging must include specificity (farm, altitude, process, cupping notes). The Guide archetype's "we tested, we stand behind" framing counters this. Consider a cupping score or review quote as social proof. |
| **3-tier system not understood at a glance** | Moderate | The tier system is the brand's structural differentiator — it must be legible immediately across any asset. If it requires explanation to parse, it fails. |
| **Freshness guarantee not believed** | Moderate | The degassing + vacuum-sealing process must be communicated as a concrete, verifiable part of the product experience — not just an abstract claim. The ≥6-week number must appear prominently. |
| **Brand feels "small" rather than "curated"** | Moderate | Confidence in voice (not arrogance) is the counter. Specifics > superlatives. "We source from Doi Chang, Chiang Rai" sounds like expertise. "The world's best beans" sounds like a small brand overreaching. |
| **Segment A (Explorer) is too small to scale the campaign** | Low-Medium | The strategy correctly prioritises Segment B (Confident Beginner) as primary. Segment A messaging is present (Pillar 3) but doesn't carry the conversion burden. The trio sampler serves both segments. |

## 6. Implications for Creative

This section defines what creative must achieve strategically. It does not prescribe visual execution or specific asset forms — those decisions belong to the Creative Director.

### What creative must accomplish

1. **The 3-tier system must be instantly legible.** The Confident Beginner's #1 barrier is not knowing which bean to pick. Creative must communicate the tier structure (safe → complex → exciting) in a way that requires zero explanation. If a customer has to read a paragraph to understand the tiers, the creative has failed. This is the single highest-consequence creative challenge in the campaign.

2. **Freshness must feel concrete, not claimed.** The ≥6-week guarantee is Coast Coffee's most powerful trust-builder and the answer to the #1 customer objection ("will the beans be fresh?"). Creative must make the guarantee feel real and verifiable — an experience the customer can see or feel, not a line they have to take on faith. The degassing and vacuum-sealing process is the proof.

3. **Asian origins must be named, not gestured at.** The quality-perception headwind (Research §4.2) is countered by specificity. Creative must include concrete origin details — farm names, regions, altitudes, processes. Generic "Asian beans" language reinforces the very perception the strategy is designed to overcome.

4. **The trio sampler is the hero offer.** All creative should lead customers toward the trio purchase. It is the lowest-friction entry point, the best demonstration of the 3-tier system, and the strongest bridge to repeat purchase. Single-bag purchases are secondary.

### Core message

> **"Asia's best beans, your perfect match."**

### Strategic constraints

- **Brand voice (see §3.3):** Curious, warm, confident — the well-travelled friend who knows coffee. Every Don't in §3.3 comes with concrete banned phrases; brief accordingly.
- **Every piece of creative must ground itself in at least one messaging pillar (see §2.2).** The pillar must be identifiable in the work — not just the headline, but the persuasion architecture.
- **The 3-tier identity system must be consistent across all assets.** Tier 1, Tier 2, and Tier 3 are structural brand elements, not campaign-level decorations. Whatever identity the Creative Director assigns them must repeat everywhere.
- **The freshness guarantee (≥6 weeks) is a hard claim — never alter, inflate, or bury it.**
