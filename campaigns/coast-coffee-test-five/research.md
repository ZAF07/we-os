### 2.2 Competitive Landscape Summary

**Positioning whitespace for Coast Coffee:**

1. **No competitor leads with Asian-origin focus.** Every competitor above carries African and Latin American origins as their primary range. Asian origins (Indonesia, Thailand, China, etc.) appear as occasional single-origin offerings — never as the brand's identity. This is Coast Coffee's clearest differentiation.

2. **No competitor uses a simple 3-tier flavour-adventurousness system.** Hook Coffee has a quiz, CMCR has "Regional" vs "UnCommon," and Round Boy has "Omakase" — but none frame their range as *safe → complex → exciting* tiers that guide the customer's own adventurousness journey. This is Coast Coffee's second structural moat.

3. **Freshness guarantees are surprisingly rare as explicit marketing claims.** Only Hook Coffee ("within 7 days of roasting") and Tiong Hoe ("roast twice a week, ship within 5 days") publish delivery cadences. Coast Coffee's ≥6-week *guaranteed shelf life* (post-degassing + vacuum-sealing) is a different and potentially more compelling angle — it's about how long the beans *stay* fresh, not just how quickly they ship.

4. **Price positioning: Coast Coffee (SGD 18–30) sits in the premium mid-range.** Above Hook ($14+) and Tiong Hoe capsules ($14.50+), competitive with Nylon ($25–28.50), PPP ($17–28.50), and Yahava ($21+). The trio bundle at ~SGD 50–54 (~$17–18/bag) would be price-competitive for a sampler.

5. **Subscription models are table stakes.** Nearly every competitor offers one. Coast Coffee's differentiation must come from *what* is curated (Asian origins × 3-tier flavour framework), not the subscription mechanic itself.

### 2.3 Indirect Competitors & Substitutes
- **Supermarket specialty beans** (e.g., Starbucks, illy, Lavazza at NTUC/Cold Storage) — cheaper, mass-market, no freshness guarantee.
- **Café-prepared specialty coffee** — the "why brew at home?" substitute; home-brewing cost savings are the counterargument.
- **General e-commerce coffee** (Shopee, Lazada, Amazon.sg) — price-competitive but no curation, freshness uncertain.
- **Coffee subscriptions from non-Singapore roasters** (international DTC shipping in) — less relevant due to shipping time/cost but worth noting.

## 5. Audience Segmentation

**Framework applied:** Need-State Segmentation + Behavioural Segmentation (who buys, why they buy, what triggers purchase).

### 5.1 Primary Segments for This Campaign

#### Segment A: "The Curious Explorer" (Tier 2–3 leaning)
- **Who:** Home barista, 25–40, brews pour-over or espresso 3–7×/week. Already buys specialty beans from established roasters. Has tried the usual origins and is bored. Wants something new.
- **Need state:** Discovery, novelty, connoisseurship signal. "I want beans my coffee friends haven't tried."
- **Trigger:** Seeing an unfamiliar origin (e.g., Yunnan, Thai Doi Chang) in their feed. Intrigued by the 3-tier system — especially Tier 3 "exciting & unknown."
- **Objection to overcome:** "Are Asian beans actually good?" → solved by tasting notes, cupping scores, origin stories.
- **Estimated size:** ~15–25% of the home barista segment.
- **Channel fit:** Instagram, specialty coffee communities, barista forums, word of mouth.

#### Segment B: "The Confident Beginner" (Tier 1–2 leaning)
- **Who:** Brews at home 2–5×/week, owns a V60/French Press/Aeropress. Knows they like specialty coffee but finds choosing beans overwhelming. Wants reliable quality without study.
- **Need state:** Simplification, reassurance, convenience. "Just tell me what I'll like."
- **Trigger:** The 3-tier system — Tier 1 "basic & safe" is the entry point. The trio sampler is perfect for this segment.
- **Objection to overcome:** "Why pay SGD 18–30 when supermarket is cheaper?" → solved by freshness promise, flavour quality, and curation.
- **Estimated size:** ~35–50% of the home barista segment — likely the largest group.
- **Channel fit:** Meta Ads, Google Search ("buy coffee beans Singapore delivery"), content marketing.

#### Segment C: "The Convenience-First Buyer" (all tiers)
- **Who:** Loves specialty coffee but time-poor. Used to buying beans from a café but can't always make the trip. Delivery is the primary value prop.
- **Need state:** Convenience without quality compromise. "I want café-quality beans at my door."
- **Trigger:** Ad highlighting delivery + freshness guarantee. "Fresh beans, delivered, stay fresh ≥6 weeks."
- **Objection to overcome:** "Will they be fresh when they arrive?" → solved by degassing + vacuum-sealing story.
- **Estimated size:** ~25–35% of the home barista segment.
- **Channel fit:** Meta Ads (retargeting), Google Search, shopping ads.

### 5.2 Segment Priority for This Campaign

Given the **70-new-customer / SGD 1,000 budget / 8-week** constraint:

| Priority | Segment | Rationale |
|----------|---------|-----------|
| **1 (Primary)** | Segment B: Confident Beginner | Largest addressable pool; trio sampler is a natural fit; the 3-tier system most directly solves their pain point. Lower acquisition friction — they're actively looking for guidance. |

## 6. Research Synthesis — Key Implications for Campaign Strategy

> **Note:** The Market Research Agent does not make strategic recommendations — that is the Brand Strategy Agent's role. The following are evidence summaries the downstream agents will build on.

1. **The Asian-origin positioning is a genuine whitespace.** No major Singapore DTC roaster leads with Asia-first sourcing. This is Coast Coffee's most defensible differentiation — but it requires education to overcome the "are Asian beans good?" perception.

2. **The 3-tier flavour system is structurally distinctive.** While competitors have some curation (quizzes, omakase, tiers), none use the *safe → complex → exciting* adventurousness gradient that Coast Coffee proposes. This is the second pillar of differentiation and directly serves the largest segment (Confident Beginner).

3. **The freshness guarantee (≥6 weeks) is an underused claim in the market.** Most competitors emphasise roast-to-ship speed, not post-delivery shelf life. Coast Coffee's degassing + vacuum-sealing story is unique and addresses a real anxiety.

4. **The trio sampler is a strategically sound launch offer.** At ~SGD 50–54 for 3 bags, it's price-competitive (lower per-bag than buying individually), lets customers experience the full 3-tier system, and reduces first-purchase risk. It's a natural bridge from "curious" to "committed."

5. **The competitive field is crowded but undifferentiated on origin.** Standing out requires Coast Coffee to lean hard into what competitors *can't* easily copy: Asian sourcing relationships, the 3-tier curation philosophy, and the freshness process.

6. **Budget constraint (SGD 1,000) means paid media must be surgically targeted.** At a target CAC of ≤SGD 14 (blended), paid channels need to deliver ~35–45 customers. This likely means Meta Ads (lookalike + interest-based targeting on coffee/brewing), not broad-reach campaigns.
