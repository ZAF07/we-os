## 1. Creative Concept: "Your Perfect Match"

### 1.1 The Idea

Specialty coffee shouldn't feel like homework. Coast Coffee's entire proposition — the 3-tier system, the Asian origins, the freshness guarantee — exists to make one thing true: **there is a bean here that was meant for you, and finding it is simple.**

"Your Perfect Match" is both a promise and an invitation. It says: we've done the sourcing, the testing, the curation. You bring your preference. We'll find the match. The trio sampler is the physical proof — three tiers, one box, your match inside.

### 1.2 Why This Concept

| Reason | Grounding |
|--------|-----------|
| **Directly serves the primary segment** | Segment B (Confident Beginner) wants beans matched to taste without guesswork (Research §1.1, JTBD #1). "Your Perfect Match" is the answer to that exact need — not "here are 47 origins, good luck" but "start here, find yours." |
| **Works across all messaging pillars** | Pillar 1: the 3-tier system matches beans to YOUR taste. Pillar 2: freshness that matches your timeline (≥6 weeks — brew when you want). Pillar 3: Asian origins that match your curiosity. Pillar 4: delivery that matches your convenience. |
| **Warm, not clinical** | The brand voice is "curious, warm, confident — a well-travelled friend who knows coffee" (Brand Strategy §3.3). "Your Perfect Match" has the right tone: personal, assured, a little charming. It's not algorithmic ("optimal bean selection"), not pretentious ("your palate deserves"), not needy ("please try us"). |
| **Lifts directly from the core message** | Core message: "Asia's best beans, your perfect match" (Brand Strategy §2.1). The concept is the message — no translation required. |
| **The trio sampler is the perfect vehicle** | The trio is three chances to find your match in one purchase. It's the lowest-friction way to experience the concept's promise. Single-bag purchases ask for commitment; the trio asks for curiosity. |
| **Differentiates without attacking** | No competitor uses "match" language this way. Hook Coffee has a quiz (algorithmic matching — feels like a test). Round Boy has omakase (they choose for you — you're passive). "Your Perfect Match" positions the customer as active ("you choose your adventurousness") while Coast Coffee does the behind-the-scenes work ("we sourced, tested, and organised"). |

### 1.3 The Creative Territory: "Warm Precision"

The creative lives at the intersection of two qualities that usually don't coexist in coffee marketing:

| Quality | What It Looks Like | What It Rejects |
|---------|-------------------|-----------------|
| **Warm** | Tactile, sensory, human. The beans, the hands that brew, the steam rising from a pour-over. Earth tones, natural light, honest textures. | Cold studio lighting, sterile product-on-white, corporate polish that feels like a stock photo. |
| **Precise** | Specific origins named. The 3-tier system shown clearly. The ≥6-week guarantee visible. Degassing and vacuum-sealing made tangible. | Vague "premium quality" language. Beans that could be from anywhere. Aesthetic without information. |

This territory is visually and tonally distinct from:
- **Hook Coffee's quirky/colourful/pop-art** aesthetic — Coast Coffee is warmer and more origin-grounded.
- **Nylon's minimal/architectural** aesthetic — Coast Coffee is more approachable, less austere.
- **PPP's heritage/industrial** aesthetic — Coast Coffee is fresher, less nostalgia-driven.

### 1.4 How the Concept Serves the Business Objective

The business objective is 70 new customers in 8 weeks. "Your Perfect Match" serves this in three ways:

1. **Reduces purchase anxiety.** The Confident Beginner's core barrier is "what if I pick the wrong bean?" The concept reframes the purchase: you're not picking a bean — you're starting a match. The trio makes it impossible to pick wrong.

2. **Creates a repeat-purchase bridge.** "Find your match" implies a journey with a destination. Once a customer brews all three tiers and identifies their favourite, reordering is the natural next step. The concept builds the retention mechanism into the acquisition.

3. **Generates word-of-mouth.** "I found my perfect match" is something people say. It's shareable — more so than "I bought some coffee beans." The concept carries social currency.

## 2. Phase 1: Creative Variants (Paid Social)

Three variants for Meta feed and story ads, each testing a different pillar combination. All three lead to the trio sampler purchase. The goal of Phase 1 testing is to identify which pillar combination drives the lowest CPA for the primary segment (Confident Beginner).

### 2.1 Variant A — "No Guesswork"

**The insight:** The Confident Beginner's #1 pain point is choice overwhelm (Research §1.3: "Overwhelming choice — too many origins, roasters, flavour notes"). This variant frames the 3-tier system as the antidote: simple, visual, no reading required.

| Attribute | Detail |
|-----------|--------|
| **Lead pillars** | Pillar 1 (Pick Your Flavour, Not a Mystery) + Pillar 2 (Freshness You Can Count On) |
| **Primary segment** | Segment B: Confident Beginner |
| **Secondary segment** | Segment C: Convenience-First Buyer |
| **Creative territory** | The 3-tier gradient as visual hero. Warm precision in its clearest form. |
| **Persuasion architecture** | "You know what you like. We organised the beans so you can act on it." → Show the tiers → Show the trio → Show the freshness guarantee → CTA: "Find your match." |
| **Tone** | Reassuring, simple, confident. The Guide archetype leads. |

#### Concept execution

The creative must communicate the 3-tier system **at a glance** — no paragraph of explanation. The tiers should be a visual gradient the viewer understands in under 3 seconds.

**Feed ad (video, 15–30 sec):**
- 0–3 sec: Hook — "Not sure which beans you'll like?" Text overlay on a shot of three distinct coffee bags or beans side by side, clearly differentiated by colour/treatment.
- 3–8 sec: The tier reveal — three bags arranged left to right. Tier 1: warm, approachable, labelled "Safe & Comforting." Tier 2: more vibrant, labelled "Complex & Floral." Tier 3: rich, intriguing, labelled "Exciting & Unknown."
- 8–15 sec: The trio box — all three bags in one package. "Try all three. Find your match." Price visible (~SGD 50–54).
- 15–20 sec: Freshness seal — the ≥6-week guarantee badge/icon. "Vacuum-sealed. Guaranteed fresh for 6+ weeks."
- 20–30 sec: CTA — "Your perfect match is in this box. Order the trio."

**Feed ad (static):**
- A single image or carousel showing the three tiers as a clear, visually distinct gradient. Tier names visible. Trio box in frame. Freshness guarantee badge present. Headline: "Three tiers. Your choice. Guaranteed fresh." CTA: "Shop the Trio."

**Story ad (vertical, 9:16):**
- Fast-cut vertical format. Same information hierarchy compressed to 8–12 seconds. Swipe-up CTA: "Find Your Match."

#### What success looks like for Variant A

This variant should resonate with the largest segment (Confident Beginner). It answers the "what do I pick?" question directly. If it underperforms, the 3-tier system may need clearer visual expression — the Creative Director should re-evaluate whether the gradient is legible instantly.

### 2.2 Variant B — "Freshness You Can See"

**The insight:** The #1 inferred objection from the DNA is "Will the beans actually be fresh when they arrive?" (DNA + Research §1.3). This variant leads with the degassing + vacuum-sealing process and the ≥6-week guarantee — making freshness tangible, not just claimed. This is Coast Coffee's most defensible trust-builder because no competitor makes the same claim in the same way (Research §2.2, Pillar 3 of competitive whitespace).

| Attribute | Detail |
|-----------|--------|
| **Lead pillar** | Pillar 2 (Freshness You Can Count On) — dominant |
| **Supporting pillar** | Pillar 4 (Delivered to Your Door) — hygiene |
| **Primary segment** | Segment C: Convenience-First Buyer (freshness anxiety) |
| **Secondary segment** | Segment B: Confident Beginner (trust-building) |
| **Creative territory** | Process as proof. Show the vacuum seal, the degassing, the guarantee. Warm precision via transparency. |
| **Persuasion architecture** | "Freshness isn't a promise. It's a process." → Show the degassing → Show the vacuum seal → Show the ≥6-week guarantee → Show the trio → CTA: "Fresh beans, your match, delivered." |
| **Tone** | Confident, precise, transparent. The Guide archetype with technical credibility. |

#### Concept execution

This is the trust-building variant. The degassing and vacuum-sealing process is the proof — it's concrete, visual, and unique to Coast Coffee (no competitor tells this story — Research §2.2). The creative must make the viewer feel the freshness, not just read about it.

**Feed ad (video, 15–30 sec):**
- 0–3 sec: Hook — a close-up of a vacuum-sealed bag being opened, the "whoosh" of the seal breaking. Text: "This bag has been fresh for 4 weeks. It'll stay fresh for 2 more. Here's why."
- 3–10 sec: The process — degassing (beans resting, releasing CO₂), then vacuum-sealing (the machine, the seal, the final product). No voiceover, just ASMR-style sound design and clean text overlays. "We degas every batch. Then we vacuum-seal it. The result: beans that stay fresh for at least 6 weeks after delivery."
- 10–18 sec: The guarantee — "≥6 weeks guaranteed freshness" as a prominent badge. "Not 'roasted fast, shipped fast.' Actually fresh when you brew it."
- 18–25 sec: The trio box — the freshness guarantee applies to all three tiers. "Try all three. Freshness guaranteed on every bag."
- 25–30 sec: CTA — "Fresh beans, your perfect match. Order the trio."

**Feed ad (static):**
- Hero image: a vacuum-sealed bag of Coast Coffee beans, the seal clearly visible, light catching the packaging. Text overlay: "Vacuum-sealed. Guaranteed fresh for 6+ weeks. Delivered." The ≥6-week badge is prominent. Secondary text: "Find your match — try the trio." CTA: "Shop Now."

**Story ad (vertical, 9:16):**
- Quick-hit process sequence: seal → guarantee → trio → CTA. 8–12 seconds. The visual of the vacuum seal breaking or the sealed bag is the hook frame.

#### What success looks like for Variant B

This variant should convert viewers who have been burned by stale mail-order beans before — or who are sceptical of a new brand's quality. If it outperforms Variant A, freshness anxiety is the dominant barrier to purchase, and the guarantee should feature more prominently across all creative in Phase 2. If it underperforms, freshness may be table stakes that customers expect but don't lead their decision.

### 2.3 Variant C — "Asia, In Your Cup"

**The insight:** Coast Coffee's Asian-origin focus is its most structurally defensible differentiation — no competitor leads with it (Research §2.2, Pillar 1). But it carries a headwind: "Are Asian beans as good as the origins I know?" (Brand Strategy §5). This variant meets that scepticism with specificity — naming farms, regions, and tasting notes — and wraps the unfamiliar in the familiar comfort of the 3-tier system.

| Attribute | Detail |
|-----------|--------|
| **Lead pillar** | Pillar 3 (Asia in Your Cup) — dominant |
| **Supporting pillar** | Pillar 1 (Pick Your Flavour, Not a Mystery) |
| **Primary segment** | Segment A: Curious Explorer (novelty-seeking) |
| **Secondary segment** | Segment B: Confident Beginner (the tiers make discovery safe) |
| **Creative territory** | Origin storytelling with specificity. Maps, farm names, bean close-ups, tasting notes. The Explorer archetype leads, with the Guide providing the framework. |
| **Persuasion architecture** | "Your coffee friends probably haven't tried these." → Name specific origins (Yunnan, Doi Chang, Sumatra, etc.) → Show the beans, the tasting notes → Show the 3-tier framework that makes them approachable → Trio as the discovery vehicle → CTA: "Taste Asia's best. Find your match." |
| **Tone** | Curious, warm, knowledgeable. The Explorer archetype — "we found this, we tested it, we think you'll love it." |

#### Concept execution

The creative must name specific origins — not "Asian beans" but "Yunnan, China" and "Doi Chang, Chiang Rai, Thailand." Specifics counter the quality-perception headwind (Brand Strategy §5: "Asian beans = lower quality"). The 3-tier system frames the origins as a journey: Tier 1 is your familiar starting point, Tier 3 is the destination.

**Feed ad (video, 15–30 sec):**
- 0–3 sec: Hook — a map of Asia with pins on Yunnan (China), Doi Chang (Thailand), Sumatra (Indonesia). Text overlay: "Some of the world's most interesting beans come from here. You probably haven't tried them yet."
- 3–12 sec: Origin showcase — quick cuts of each origin: bean close-ups, farm/landscape shots (could be stock or brand-provided), tasting notes on screen. "Yunnan, China: strawberry, white tea, clean finish. Doi Chang, Thailand: jasmine, stone fruit, honey. Sumatra, Indonesia: dark chocolate, cedar, full body."
- 12–18 sec: The framework — the 3-tier system appears. "We organise them so you can explore at your own pace. Tier 1: safe & comforting. Tier 2: complex & floral. Tier 3: exciting & unknown."
- 18–25 sec: The trio — "One box, three origins, your perfect match." Trio price visible.
- 25–30 sec: CTA — "Start your journey. Order the trio."

**Feed ad (static):**
- Carousel format ideal: Card 1 — a specific origin with farm name, region, and tasting note. Card 2 — second origin. Card 3 — third origin. Card 4 — the trio box, "Find your match." Card 5 — freshness guarantee. Each card is visually distinct, origin-grounded. Headline: "Yunnan. Doi Chang. Sumatra. Your next favourite bean is from somewhere you haven't looked."

**Story ad (vertical, 9:16):**
- Origin-focused, fast cuts. Map → bean → tasting note → tier → trio. 8–12 seconds. The specific origin names are the hook.

#### What success looks like for Variant C

This variant tests whether origin curiosity converts at a competitive CPA. If it outperforms Variants A and B, the Asian-origin story is the strongest acquisition lever — and Phase 2 creative should lead with origin specificity. If it underperforms, origin is a supporting differentiator (it validates the choice after the decision, rather than driving the decision itself) and should play a secondary role in Phase 2 creative.

## 3. Organic Content Direction

Organic social (Instagram + TikTok) serves two strategic purposes (Campaign Strategy §2.1):
1. **Social proof** — the destination for paid visitors who check the brand before purchasing.
2. **Algorithmic discovery** — zero-cost reach to Singapore coffee-interested audiences.

Organic content does not need to sell directly. It needs to make Coast Coffee look like a brand worth buying from — real, knowledgeable, and consistent.

### 3.1 Content Themes

Five content themes, organised by strategic function. Every organic post should belong to one of these themes.

#### Theme 1: Origin Stories (Explorer)
**Purpose:** Differentiate Coast Coffee through origin specificity. Build the Asian-origin brand identity. Serve Segment A (Curious Explorer) organically.

**Content:** Deep dives into specific origins — farm name, region, altitude, process, tasting notes. "This week's Tier 2: a washed anaerobic from Doi Chang, Chiang Rai. Jasmine, stone fruit, honey. Here's the farm it came from."

**Formats:** Carousel posts (IG), photo + caption storytelling (IG), 30–60 sec origin videos (TikTok/Reels).

**Frequency:** 1–2× per week.

**Why this matters:** Every origin post is a brick in the wall against the "Asian beans = lower quality" headwind (Brand Strategy §5). Specificity = credibility.

#### Theme 2: The Process (Guide)
**Purpose:** Make the freshness guarantee tangible. Build trust through transparency. Address the #1 objection ("will they be fresh?").

**Content:** Behind-the-scenes of degassing, vacuum-sealing, testing, cupping. "This is what degassing looks like. This is why we do it. This is what the vacuum seal does."

**Formats:** Short-form video (TikTok/Reels) — ASMR-style, process-focused, minimal talking. Photo + caption on IG.

**Frequency:** 1× per week.

**Why this matters:** No competitor shows this process (Research §2.2). It's visual, distinctive, and trust-building content that only Coast Coffee can make.

#### Theme 3: Brew Guides (Guide)
**Purpose:** Serve the Confident Beginner with practical, helpful content. Build the Guide archetype. Create saveable, shareable content.

**Content:** "How to brew Tier 1 with a V60." "French Press vs. Pour-Over — which suits your tier?" "3 mistakes home baristas make with fresh beans." Educational, warm, never condescending.

**Formats:** Carousel how-to guides (IG), 30–90 sec tutorial videos (TikTok/Reels).

**Frequency:** 1× per week.

**Why this matters:** Brew guides are high-save-rate content (bookmarked for later). They position Coast Coffee as helpful, not salesy. They also serve the Confident Beginner's core need: "help me brew better coffee."

#### Theme 4: Tier Reveals / Unboxing (Both Archetypes)
**Purpose:** Showcase the product. Make the trio sampler desirable. Create anticipation for what's in the box.

**Content:** "What's in this month's trio?" Unboxing videos. Tier-by-tier tasting. "I brewed all three — here's which one was my match."

**Formats:** Unboxing Reel/TikTok (15–45 sec), carousel with tasting notes (IG), side-by-side brewing comparison (TikTok).

**Frequency:** 1× per week (align with any restocks or new tier rotations).

**Why this matters:** This is the closest organic content gets to selling — but it sells through demonstration, not persuasion. "Look what you get" beats "you should buy this."

#### Theme 5: Community & UGC (Guide + Explorer)
**Purpose:** Social proof. Show real people enjoying Coast Coffee. Encourage sharing. Build the referral flywheel.

**Content:** Re-sharing customer brews (with permission). "Tag us in your morning brew." Community questions: "What's your go-to brewing method?" "Tier 1, 2, or 3 — which are you this week?"

**Formats:** Story reshares (IG), duets/stitches (TikTok), community response posts.

**Frequency:** Phase 1: 1× per week (seed with early customers + community group connections). Phase 2: 2–3× per week as customer base grows (Campaign Strategy §1.3).

**Why this matters:** Community content closes the trust gap for a new brand. When a paid-ad viewer visits the IG profile and sees real people brewing Coast Coffee, the objection "can I trust a new, small brand?" weakens.

### 3.2 Platform Strategy

#### Instagram (Primary Organic Channel)

| Attribute | Direction |
|-----------|-----------|
| **Role** | Brand home. The "storefront" paid visitors check before purchasing. |
| **Feed aesthetic** | Warm precision: earth tones (browns, creams, deep greens), natural light, tactile textures (beans, burlap, paper, ceramic). The 3-tier colour system should be consistent across all posts — Tier 1 warm/amber, Tier 2 vibrant/emerald-leaning, Tier 3 deep/indigo-leaning. |
| **Feed content mix** | Origin Stories (25%), Brew Guides (25%), Tier Reveals (25%), The Process (15%), Community (10%). |

|-----------|-----------|
| **Role** | Discovery engine. Algorithm-driven reach to Singapore coffee-interested users at zero media cost. |
| **Content style** | More casual, more experimental than IG. TikTok rewards authenticity and process content. Lean into coffee ASMR (brewing sounds, bean pouring, vacuum seal opening), quick origin facts, brewing experiments. |

### 3.3 Visual Identity System

Consistency across paid and organic is non-negotiable. The 3-tier identity system (Brand Strategy §6: "Tier 1, Tier 2, and Tier 3 are structural brand elements, not campaign-level decorations. Whatever identity the Creative Director assigns them must repeat everywhere.") is the foundation.

#### Tier Colour & Identity System

| Tier | Name | Colour Palette | Visual Feel | Icon/Motif |
|------|------|---------------|-------------|------------|
| **Tier 1** | Safe & Comforting | Warm amber, cream, soft brown | Approachable, familiar, morning light | A single filled cup, steam rising |
| **Tier 2** | Complex & Floral | Deep green, jade, golden accent | Vibrant, layered, garden-fresh | Blooming branch or layered leaves |
| **Tier 3** | Exciting & Unknown | Rich indigo, dark violet, copper accent | Intriguing, deep, evening/mystery | A compass or a map pin |

These colours and motifs must appear consistently across every asset — paid ads, organic posts, packaging, the landing page. The tiers should be identifiable by colour alone, even at small scale (story ad, mobile feed).

#### Typography & Layout

- **Primary typeface:** A warm, readable serif or semi-serif for headlines — something that feels editorial but approachable (not luxury, not quirky). Georgia, Lora, or a similar warm serif.
- **Secondary typeface:** A clean sans-serif for body text, guarantee badges, and the ≥6-week number — something precise but not cold. Inter, Source Sans, or similar.
- **The guarantee badge:** A distinct, recognisable mark — a seal or stamp shape containing "≥6 WEEKS FRESHNESS GUARANTEED" — that appears on every product-related asset. This is a trust signal that must become associated with the brand through repetition.
- **Logo placement:** Consistent position across all assets (bottom-right or centred-bottom). Subtle, not dominant — the beans, the tiers, and the people are the heroes.

## 4. Creative KPI Definitions & Benchmarks

Per the campaign strategy (§4.3 and §7.2), the Creative Director owns creative KPI selection and benchmarking. These KPIs are leading indicators — they signal whether creative will convert before purchase data fully accumulates. The Performance Marketing Agent tracks these against downstream Marketing KPIs (CTR ≥ 1.0%, CPA ≤ SGD 25).

### 4.1 Paid Social Creative KPIs

| KPI | Definition | Benchmark | Why This Benchmark | Measurement |
|-----|-----------|-----------|-------------------|-------------|
| **Hook rate** | 3-second video views ÷ impressions | ≥ 25% | The goal document sets this baseline. Industry average for F&B Meta video is 20–30%. Below 25% means the opening frame/line isn't stopping the scroll — the creative fails before the message lands. | Meta Ads Manager (3-second video view metric) |
| **Video completion rate (15 sec)** | Views reaching 15 seconds ÷ total impressions | ≥ 20% | For 15–30 sec ads, this measures whether the creative holds attention through the persuasion arc. Dropping below 20% suggests the middle section (message delivery) is losing viewers. | Meta Ads Manager (video % watched) |
| **CTR (link click → LP)** | Link clicks ÷ impressions | ≥ 1.0% | Inherited from Marketing KPIs (Campaign Strategy §4.2). This is the bridge metric between creative performance and conversion. | Meta Ads Manager (CTR — link click) |
| **Thumb-stop rate (static)** | Clicks (all) ÷ impressions on static ads | ≥ 1.5% | Static ads typically have lower CTR than video. 1.5% is the floor for a well-composed static with a clear CTA. | Meta Ads Manager (CTR — all) |

**KPI hierarchy for paid creative:** Hook rate is the lead creative KPI — if people don't stop, nothing else matters. Completion rate and CTR are secondary — they measure whether the stopped viewer becomes an interested prospect. If hook rate ≥ 25% but completion rate < 15%, the middle of the creative needs tightening. If completion rate ≥ 20% but CTR < 1.0%, the CTA or offer isn't compelling.

### 4.2 Organic Social Creative KPIs

| KPI | Definition | Benchmark | Why This Benchmark | Measurement |
|-----|-----------|-----------|-------------------|-------------|
| **Engagement rate (IG feed)** | (Likes + comments + saves + shares) ÷ reach | ≥ 4% | Goal document baseline. Singapore F&B IG accounts average 1.5–3%. 4% is ambitious for a new account but achievable with high-quality, saveable content (brew guides, origin stories). | Instagram Insights |
| **Save rate (IG feed)** | Saves ÷ reach | ≥ 2% | Saves signal valuable, reference-worthy content. Brew guides and tier breakdowns should drive saves. This is the strongest organic signal that content is serving the Guide archetype well. | Instagram Insights |
| **Share rate (IG feed)** | Shares ÷ reach | ≥ 1.5% | Shares are the organic referral engine. Origin stories and tier reveals should be shareable. | Instagram Insights |
| **Engagement rate (TikTok)** | (Likes + comments + shares + favourites) ÷ views | ≥ 5% | TikTok benchmarks run higher than IG. F&B content can hit 5–8%. Below 5% means the content isn't resonating with the algorithm or audience. | TikTok Analytics |
| **Video completion rate (TikTok)** | Full video views ÷ total views | ≥ 30% | TikTok completion rates are typically higher than Meta. 30% is the floor for content that holds attention. Process/ASMR content should exceed this. | TikTok Analytics |
| **Follower growth rate (both platforms)** | New followers ÷ total followers, weekly | ≥ 5% (Phase 1), ≥ 10% (Phase 2) | Growth rate is a lagging indicator of content quality and consistency. Phase 2 target is higher because the base is small and paid is driving profile visits. | Platform native analytics |

**KPI hierarchy for organic creative:** Save rate is the lead creative KPI for Instagram — it measures whether the Guide content is genuinely useful. Engagement rate is the lead for TikTok — it determines algorithmic distribution. Share rate on both platforms is the organic multiplier — it's the closest proxy for word-of-mouth.

### 4.3 The Creative-to-Marketing KPI Chain

```
CREATIVE KPIs (Leading)
├── Hook rate ≥ 25%         ──→  drives  ──→  CTR ≥ 1.0%
├── Completion rate ≥ 20%   ──→  drives  ──→  Landing page visits
├── Save rate ≥ 2%          ──→  drives  ──→  Brand trust / return visits
└── Share rate ≥ 1.5%       ──→  drives  ──→  Organic reach / referral

MARKETING KPIs
├── CTR ≥ 1.0%              ──→  drives  ──→  Landing page sessions
└── Landing page CVR ≥ 3%   ──→  drives  ──→  Cost per purchase

BUSINESS KPI
└── CPA ≤ SGD 25            ──→  delivers  ──→  70 new customers
```

If creative KPIs are met but Marketing KPIs aren't (e.g., hook rate is 28% but CTR is 0.7%), the problem is likely in the landing page or offer, not the creative. If creative KPIs aren't met (e.g., hook rate is 15%), the creative needs reworking regardless of downstream metrics.

### 4.4 Creative Optimisation Triggers (Phase 1 → Phase 2)

| Trigger | Action |
|---------|--------|
| Any variant achieves CPA ≤ SGD 20 within first 2 weeks | That variant is the Phase 2 winner. Scale it immediately — don't wait for Week 4. |
| All variants have hook rate < 20% by end of Week 2 | Creative direction problem. Re-brief: the opening frame/hook isn't working. Produce 2 new hooks across the best-performing variant's body. |
| One variant has CPA > SGD 35 by end of Week 2 | Kill it. Reallocate budget to the remaining variants. |
| Variant C (Asia) has CPA ≤ SGD 25 but Variant A (No Guesswork) has CPA > SGD 30 | The origin story converts better than the tier story. Phase 2 should lead with origin, with the tiers as framework. |
| Variant B (Freshness) has CPA ≤ SGD 25 | Freshness anxiety is real and the process story converts. Amplify the guarantee badge and process content in Phase 2. |

## 5. Phase 2 Creative Evolution

Phase 2 (Weeks 5–8) creative depends on Phase 1 data. This section defines the creative response to each likely outcome, so the transition is fast.

### 5.1 If Variant A ("No Guesswork") Wins

**Implication:** The Confident Beginner's #1 driver is the 3-tier system's simplicity. They want guidance, not origin stories.

**Phase 2 creative direction:**
- Scale the winning Variant A creative as-is.
- Produce 2 new variants that explore the 3-tier system from different angles:
  - "Which tier are you?" — a personality-driven variant (quiz-like, without being a quiz). "If you drink the same thing every day → Tier 1. If you have a go-to but get curious → Tier 2. If you never order the same thing twice → Tier 3."
  - "The tier journey" — a customer-journey variant. "Week 1: Tier 1. Week 3: Tier 2. Week 6: Tier 3. Find your match at your own pace."
- Increase organic content focused on tier spotlights and brew guides (Theme 3 + Theme 4).
- Organic TikTok: "Which tier am I brewing today?" daily series.

### 5.2 If Variant B ("Freshness You Can See") Wins

**Implication:** Freshness anxiety is the dominant purchase barrier. The process story converts sceptics into customers.

**Phase 2 creative direction:**
- Scale the winning Variant B creative.
- Produce 2 new variants deepening the freshness story:
  - "The 6-week guarantee: what it means" — educational variant. "Week 1: peak flavour. Week 3: still developing. Week 6: still fresh — here's the science."
  - "Unboxing freshness" — experience variant. A customer receives the trio, opens the vacuum-sealed bag, brews immediately. Real reaction, real freshness.
- Increase organic process content (Theme 2). Make the vacuum seal and degassing visually iconic — something people associate with Coast Coffee.
- The freshness guarantee badge should be more prominent across all assets in Phase 2.

### 5.3 If Variant C ("Asia, In Your Cup") Wins

**Implication:** Origin curiosity is a stronger conversion driver than expected. The Explorer archetype leads.

**Phase 2 creative direction:**
- Scale the winning Variant C creative.
- Produce 2 new variants deepening the origin story:
  - "Meet the farm" — origin-deep-dive variant. One ad per origin, each a mini-documentary (30 sec): the farm, the altitude, the process, the tasting notes.
  - "Asia vs. the world" — comparison variant (tastefully, no competitor names). "You know Ethiopian Yirgacheffe. You know Colombian Huila. Now meet Yunnan — strawberry, white tea, clean finish."
- Increase organic origin story content (Theme 1). Build a library of origin-specific content.
- The 3-tier system becomes the framework that makes origins approachable rather than the lead message.

### 5.4 If No Variant Clears SGD 25 CPA

Per Campaign Strategy §5: pause paid, re-brief Creative Director with performance data, produce 3 new variants.

**Possible creative failure modes and responses:**

| Failure Mode | Symptom | Creative Response |
|-------------|---------|-------------------|
| Hook fails across all variants | Hook rate < 18% on all | Re-brief hooks. The current openings aren't stopping the scroll. Test: a bold question hook ("Still buying supermarket beans?"), a surprising fact hook ("Most coffee goes stale in 2 weeks — ours doesn't"), a visual hook (extreme close-up of vacuum seal breaking). |
| Message lands but offer doesn't convert | Hook rate ≥ 25%, completion rate ≥ 20%, but CTR < 0.8% | The trio offer isn't compelling in the ad. Test: make the price/value explicit in the creative ("3 bags, ~SGD 17 each"), add a limited-time framing ("Launch trio — available this month only"), or test a single-bag entry offer if trio resistance is structural. |
| Creative is fine, audience is wrong | Strong creative KPIs, weak CPA | Not a creative problem — hand back to Performance Marketing Agent to re-evaluate audience targeting. |

## 7. Creative Guardrails (Non-Negotiable)

These are the creative equivalent of the brand strategy's Do's and Don'ts. Every asset — paid, organic, email, packaging — must comply.

### Do
- **Name specific origins.** Every mention of beans must include at least the country and region (e.g., "Yunnan, China" or "Doi Chang, Chiang Rai"). Never "Asian beans" as a catch-all.
- **Show the 3-tier gradient visually.** The tiers must be identifiable by colour and position in every asset where they appear. Left → right: Tier 1 → Tier 2 → Tier 3. The gradient is the brand's visual signature.
- **Include the freshness guarantee badge on every product-related asset.** The ≥6-week number must appear. It's the trust signal.
- **Use sensory language.** Tasting notes, aroma descriptors, mouthfeel. "Strawberry, white tea, clean finish" not "fruity and bright."
- **Speak in first-person plural.** "We sourced," "We tested," "We cup every lot." The Guide archetype does the work so the customer doesn't have to.
- **Show real process.** The degassing. The vacuum seal. The cupping. Real process is more compelling than staged lifestyle photography.
- **Price the trio visibly in paid ads.** The ~SGD 50–54 price should appear — transparency builds trust and pre-qualifies traffic.

### Don't
- **No coffee snobbery or gatekeeping.** Everyone is welcome at every tier. Banned: "If you don't already know…" / "Serious coffee drinkers…" / "This one's not for beginners." Instead: "Here's what makes this one special."
- **No jargon without explanation.** Any technical term must be defined in the same breath. Banned: "This washed anaerobic delivers exceptional clarity." Instead: "This bean went through anaerobic fermentation — fermented without oxygen — which gives it an intense, clean flavour."
- **No generic "premium quality" claims without specifics.** Every quality claim needs a reason. Banned: "Premium beans, artisanal roasting, exceptional quality." Instead: "We cup every lot. If it doesn't score, it doesn't ship."
- **No over-promising.** The guarantee is ≥6 weeks — never inflate it. Banned: "The freshest beans in Singapore" / "You'll never go back to supermarket coffee." Instead: "Guaranteed fresh for at least 6 weeks after delivery."
- **No competitor bashing.** Coast Coffee's positioning differentiates without naming others. Banned: "Unlike [competitor]…" / "Most roasters ignore Asian origins…" Instead: "We built our whole range around Asian origins — it's what we're here for."
- **No stock-photo coffee.** Generic latte art, unidentified beans, models holding mugs in cafés. Every visual must be Coast Coffee's actual beans, actual process, or actual origins.
