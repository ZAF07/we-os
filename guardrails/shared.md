# Shared rubric — applies to every deliverable

Reject the deliverable if any of these are violated:

- **DNA-grounded.** Every claim and recommendation traces to the Customer DNA or
  to a stated research finding. No generic filler that could apply to any
  business. If something the DNA doesn't cover is needed, the deliverable says so
  explicitly rather than inventing it.
- **Explains the why.** Each recommendation states the reasoning behind it, not
  just the conclusion.
- **Ties to the business objective.** Every recommendation connects back to the
  campaign's stated business goal / KPI.
- **Stays in lane.** The deliverable does only its stage's job and does not skip
  ahead (no strategy in a research doc, no creative in a strategy doc, no
  generation prompts in a brief).
- **Evidence over assertion.** Strong claims are supported; assumptions are
  labelled as assumptions.
- **Specific and usable.** Concrete enough for the next stage to act on — no vague
  platitudes ("great quality", "engaging content") without substance.
- **Respects hard constraints.** Honors the DNA's constraints (compliance,
  off-limits claims, brand no-gos) and the campaign's constraints.
